## Headline

Across deep search agents, moderate listwise reranking yields larger accuracy gains than equivalent compute spent on additional reasoning — practitioners should rerank first and reason second, when the budget is tight.

## Summary

The paper studies how to allocate test-time compute in deep research agents. Using BrowseComp-Plus and a new Effective Token Cost (ETC) metric, the authors trade off model scale, reasoning effort, and reranking depth. The conclusion is unusually clean for an empirical study: reranking consistently improves both retrieval and end-to-end accuracy, and moderate reranking gives larger accuracy gains than equivalent compute spent on additional reasoning.

For a practitioner: this is the most directly actionable paper in Phase 2 for anyone running deep-search agents. The advice is concrete — when you have a compute budget, spend the marginal token on reranking, not on more reasoning. The ETC metric also gives you a way to make the trade-off quantitatively in your own pipeline.

## Specifics

- Benchmark: BrowseComp-Plus.
- New metric: Effective Token Cost (ETC) — measures the actual cost of accuracy gain.
- Tradeoffs studied: model scale x reasoning effort x reranking depth x total token cost.
- Headline: moderate reranking gives larger accuracy gains than equivalent reasoning budget.
