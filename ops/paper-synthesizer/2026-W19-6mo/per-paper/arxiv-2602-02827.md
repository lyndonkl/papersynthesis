## Headline

Col-Bandit prunes ColBERT-style late-interaction at query time as a finite-population top-K identification problem — zero-shot, drop-in, no retraining or index modification, preserves ranking fidelity.

## Summary

Multivector late-interaction retrievers (ColBERT-family) achieve SOTA quality but are expensive at query time: every candidate document requires exhaustive token-level MaxSim computation. Col-Bandit casts this as a top-K identification problem with statistical bounds — adaptively reveal only the (document, query token) MaxSim entries needed to determine the top results under tunable confidence. Unlike coarse offline pruning approaches, Col-Bandit sparsifies the interaction matrix on the fly, with no offline preprocessing, no index changes, no model retraining.

For a practitioner: if you are running ColBERT-class late-interaction in production, Col-Bandit is the lowest-friction efficiency upgrade in the kept set. Drop-in, zero-shot, no retraining. Worth piloting on your production setup with the tunable relaxation parameter dialled in.

## Specifics

- Casts late-interaction reranking as a finite-population Top-K identification problem.
- Maintains uncertainty-aware bounds over partially-observed document scores.
- Adaptively reveals only the MaxSim entries needed to determine top results.
- Drop-in: no index modification, no offline preprocessing, no model retraining.
- Benchmarks: BEIR (textual) and REAL-MM-RAG (multimodal); preserves ranking fidelity.
