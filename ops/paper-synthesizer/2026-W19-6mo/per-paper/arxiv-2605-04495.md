## Headline

CAR is the LCR follow-up: training-free reranking using generator-confidence change as the document-usefulness signal, with a query-level gate that avoids unnecessary intervention on already-confident queries — +25.4% YesNo reranker improvement under Contriever on average.

## Summary

CAR (Confidence-Aware Reranking) is the same group's follow-up to LCR. The mechanism is similar: estimate generator confidence as semantic consistency of multiple sampled answers under query-only and query+document conditions; promote documents that increase confidence, demote those that decrease it. The new piece is a query-level gate: if the model is already confident under query-only, do not rerank — preserve the baseline order. The gate avoids unnecessary intervention. On BEIR, CAR improves NDCG@5 across sparse and dense retrievers, four LLM backbones, and both LLM-based and supervised rerankers. The headline number is +25.4% on the YesNo reranker under Contriever; the paper also shows ranking gains correlate with downstream generation quality.

For a practitioner: CAR is the strongest training-free reranker in the kept set as of the window's end. The query-level gate is the practitioner-relevant addition — it means CAR adds latency only when needed, which makes it cheaper than LCR in production. If you have a working sparse or dense retriever and want a low-friction reranking layer, CAR is the lowest-risk pilot in the kept set.

## Specifics

- Mechanism: generator-confidence change as document-usefulness signal.
