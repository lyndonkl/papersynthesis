## Headline

AutoSearch lets the agent decide its own search depth via RL — a self-answering mechanism rewards minimal-sufficient depth and penalizes over-searching, defining the accuracy-efficiency frontier explicitly.

## Summary

Multi-step agentic-RAG often involves redundant search steps. Prior work limits depth as a hyperparameter, which underexplores complex questions. AutoSearch frames the question differently: there exists a minimal-sufficient search depth that depends jointly on question complexity and agent capability, and the agent itself can identify it via a self-answering mechanism — generate intermediate answers, score them, terminate when score is high enough. RL trains the agent to find this depth; reward mechanisms stabilize search behavior and improve answer quality on complex questions.

For a practitioner: AutoSearch operationalizes "stop when you know" for agentic RAG. The trade-off is real — fixed shallow depth fails complex queries; fixed deep depth wastes compute on simple ones; learned depth threads the needle. The self-answering scoring is the key practical idea — even partially adopted as a heuristic gate in your pipeline, it helps.

## Specifics

- Setting: agentic RAG with iterative retrieval.
- Mechanism: self-answering scoring identifies minimal-sufficient search depth.
- RL framework: reward at minimal-sufficient depth; penalize over-searching.
- Additional: reward mechanisms for search-behavior stability and answer quality.
- Multiple benchmarks; results show improved accuracy-efficiency trade-off.
