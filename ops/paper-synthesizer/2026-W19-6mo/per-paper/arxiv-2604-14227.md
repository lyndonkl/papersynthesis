## Headline

FRESCO documents that current re-rankers consistently prefer older, semantically richer documents over recent factually-current ones — and shows instruction tuning recovers up to +27% on evolving-knowledge queries.

## Summary

The paper introduces a benchmark — FRESCO (Factual Recency and Evolving Semantic COnflict) — for evaluating re-rankers in temporally-dynamic contexts. It pairs recency-seeking queries with historical Wikipedia revisions and tests whether re-rankers can prioritize factually-recent evidence while keeping semantic relevance. The headline finding is a consistent failure mode: existing re-rankers exhibit a strong bias toward older, semantically richer documents even when they are factually obsolete. The paper then investigates an instruction-optimization framework to mitigate the bias: identifying Pareto-optimal instructions yields up to +27% on evolving-knowledge tasks.

For a practitioner: if your application has time-varying ground truth (news, stock prices, regulatory text, anything where the right answer changes over time), this paper documents a failure mode you are silently hitting. The instruction-tuning fix is implementable today — practitioners can apply it without retraining the reranker model itself.

## Specifics

- Benchmark: FRESCO (Factual Recency and Evolving Semantic COnflict) — introduced here.
- Construction: pairs recency-seeking queries with historical Wikipedia revisions.
- Failure mode: re-rankers prefer older, semantically richer docs over recent factual ones.
- Mitigation: instruction-optimization framework, Pareto-optimal instructions.
