## Headline

A practitioner-oriented taxonomy of the retrieval-system design stack, organized as a vertical traversal — Representation Layer, Granularity Layer, Orchestration Layer, Robustness Layer — with explicit failure modes named at each level.

## Summary

The paper does not introduce a new method; it organizes the design space. The structure is a four-layer stack: Representation (loss functions, bi-encoder vs cross-encoder, geometric projection), Granularity (atomic vs hierarchical chunking, information bottlenecks), Orchestration (hierarchical retrieval, agentic decomposition, multi-stage reranking), and Robustness (domain generalization, lexical blind spots, temporal drift). At each layer the paper names the standard pitfalls.

For a practitioner: this is the orientation document — the kind of paper to keep open as a checklist when designing or auditing a retrieval pipeline. Not where you go for new techniques; where you go to make sure you have not skipped a question your design needs to answer.
