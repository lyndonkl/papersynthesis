## Headline

LatentRAG shifts both reasoning and retrieval from discrete language space to continuous latent space — generating latent tokens for thoughts and subqueries directly from hidden states in a single forward pass — eliminating the autoregressive-thought latency that defines agentic RAG.

## Summary

Agentic RAG's latency comes from autoregressive generation of long internal thoughts and subqueries — the LLM types out its reasoning token by token. LatentRAG eliminates this by shifting both reasoning and retrieval into continuous latent space: latent tokens for thoughts and subqueries are produced directly from hidden states in a single forward pass. The LLM is aligned with a dense retrieval model in the latent space, enabling retrieval over latent subquery tokens and end-to-end joint optimization. The paper also addresses interpretability by encouraging semantically meaningful latent representations.

For a practitioner: LatentRAG is the most structurally aggressive efficiency claim in the kept set — eliminate the autoregressive latency, not just compress it. This is research, not production. But if it replicates and the latent representations are stable enough across queries, it points at where high-throughput agentic RAG goes next. Track over the next 2-3 months for follow-up work.
