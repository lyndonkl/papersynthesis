## Headline

For learned-sparse retrieval, the forward index is a substantial fraction of total index size — DotVByte (a new compression algorithm tailored to inner-product computation) and StreamVByte give the best memory/accuracy/latency trade-off on MsMarco.

## Summary

Learned sparse retrieval (SPLADE-family) is now competitive with dense, but its forward index dominates total index size. The paper benchmarks integer compression techniques on this index; StreamVByte gives the best trade-off off the shelf, and the authors propose DotVByte, a new algorithm tailored to the inner-product computation that LSR uses. Experiments on MsMarco quantify the savings.

For a practitioner: if you have already adopted learned-sparse retrieval (or are about to, after LACONIC), this paper is the most direct engineering reference for keeping the index small without paying for it in latency or accuracy. Pure systems work — the kind you implement once and forget.
