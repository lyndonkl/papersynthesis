## Headline

A controlled measurement study finds: under tool-call and token budgets, hybrid lexical+dense retrieval with lightweight reranking gives the largest average accuracy gains; search-depth helps up to a small cap; completion budgets matter most on synthesis-heavy benchmarks.

## Summary

The paper is a measurement harness paper — it does not propose a new method; it measures how known design decisions trade off accuracy and cost under explicit budgets. The harness, Budget-Constrained Agentic Search (BCAS), surfaces remaining budget to the agent and gates tool use. The authors run 6 LLMs across 3 QA benchmarks (HotpotQA, etc.). Three findings: accuracy improves with additional searches up to a small cap; hybrid lexical+dense retrieval with lightweight reranking produces the largest average gain in the ablation grid; larger completion budgets are most helpful on HotpotQA-style synthesis tasks.

For a practitioner: this is the rare paper that gives you defaults rather than methods. If you are building a budgeted agentic-RAG pipeline today, the recommendation is concrete: hybrid retrieval + lightweight reranking, modest search depth, larger completion budgets only when synthesis is the bottleneck. The BCAS harness itself is reproducible, so you can re-run the measurement on your own LLM choices.
