## Headline

When agentic search wraps the retrieval layer, the dense-RAG vs GraphRAG performance gap shrinks substantially — agentic interaction implicitly compensates for the absence of explicit graph structure.

## Summary

The paper attacks a question that has been live for months: does GraphRAG's explicit graph structure still matter once an agent is doing dynamic, multi-round retrieval? The answer it documents is "less than you would think." RAGSearch is a unified benchmark that evaluates dense RAG and representative GraphRAG methods as retrieval infrastructures under agentic search, training-free and training-based, with standardized LLM backbones, retrieval budgets, and inference protocols.

For a practitioner: if you are deciding between investing in a knowledge-graph + GraphRAG pipeline vs sticking with dense-RAG-plus-agent, this paper says the second is more often the right choice. The implicit-structure-from-interaction finding is the operational point — agents close the gap that graphs were originally built to close.
