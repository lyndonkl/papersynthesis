## Headline

Corpus2Skill replaces top-k retrieval with agent navigation: distill the corpus into a hierarchical skill directory offline, then let the agent drill into branches at serve time — outperforms dense retrieval, RAPTOR, and agentic-RAG baselines on enterprise QA.

## Summary

The framing is clean: standard RAG treats the LLM as a passive consumer of search results — it never sees how the corpus is organized or what it has not yet retrieved. Corpus2Skill flips this. Offline, the corpus is iteratively clustered, an LLM writes summaries at each level, and the result is materialized as a tree of navigable "skill files." At serve time, the agent gets a bird's-eye view, drills into topic branches via progressively finer summaries, and retrieves full documents by ID. Because the hierarchy is visible, the agent can reason about where to look, backtrack, and combine evidence across branches.

For a practitioner: Corpus2Skill is the second strong "do not retrieve, do something else" paper in the kept set after Keyword Search Is All You Need. The trade-off is opposite: heavy offline cost to make serve-time navigation fast and structured. Worth piloting if your corpus is stable and your queries are exploratory; less attractive if your corpus updates frequently.

## Specifics

- Mechanism: distill corpus into a hierarchical skill directory offline; agent navigates at serve time.
- Compilation: iterative clustering + LLM-written summaries at each level.
- At serve time: bird's-eye view + drill-down + document retrieval by ID.
- Beats: dense retrieval, RAPTOR, agentic-RAG baselines on WixQA (enterprise customer-support).
- Generalization: nine RAGBench subsets reformulated as retrieval-stress benchmarks.
