## Headline

On a production-scale e-commerce knowledge-graph benchmark (STaRK), the right cross-encoder + retriever combination matters more than which family — but cross-encoders consistently lift retrieval precision over single-stage retrievers.

## Summary

The paper systematically evaluates retriever-reranker pipelines on a structured (knowledge-graph) e-commerce benchmark, STaRK SKB. The contribution is the comparative grid: multiple retrievers paired with multiple cross-encoder rerankers under one production-scale evaluation. The conclusion the abstract emphasizes is that integration with structured knowledge graphs remains underexplored, but cross-encoders reliably refine retrieval precision when added to the pipeline.

For a practitioner: if you are working with structured product/entity data (knowledge graphs, taxonomies), this paper is the most direct empirical reference for which retriever-plus-reranker combinations make sense at production scale. The headline is unsurprising — cross-encoders help — but the evidence is on a benchmark big enough to take seriously.

## Specifics

- Dataset: STaRK Semi-structured Knowledge Base (production-scale e-commerce).
- Pipeline grid: multiple retrievers x multiple cross-encoder rerankers.
- Finding: cross-encoders reliably improve retrieval precision when added.
- Caveat: "integration with structured data remains underexplored" — explicit hedge that the field does not yet have settled best practices for KG-RAG.
