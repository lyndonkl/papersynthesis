## Headline

MemReranker addresses the three specific failure modes of generic rerankers when applied to agent memory — score miscalibration, ranking degradation under temporal/causal queries, lack of dialogue-context disambiguation — via multi-teacher LLM distillation into a 0.6B/4B reranker family.

## Summary

Generic rerankers in agent-memory systems fail in three specific ways: relevance scores are miscalibrated (so threshold-based filtering is hard); ranking degrades under temporal-constraint and causal-reasoning queries; the model cannot use dialogue context for semantic disambiguation. MemReranker (built on Qwen3-Reranker, 0.6B and 4B variants) is trained to fix all three. The training pipeline combines multi-teacher pairwise comparisons (yielding calibrated soft labels), BCE pointwise distillation (well-distributed scores), and InfoNCE contrastive learning (hard-sample discrimination). Training data combines general corpora with memory-specific multi-turn data.

For a practitioner: this paper is operator-permitted in scope (the override allows agent-memory papers that teach a generalizable reranker insight). The reasoning-awareness gap MemReranker identifies — generic rerankers fail under temporal and causal queries — is general; you will hit it whenever your reranker has to handle anything beyond simple semantic similarity. If you are building an agent with memory, MemReranker is the most direct pilot in the kept set; if you are building any agent at all, the diagnostic is worth knowing.
