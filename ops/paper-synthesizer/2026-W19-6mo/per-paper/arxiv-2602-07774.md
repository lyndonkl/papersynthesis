## Headline

GR2 brings RL-distilled reasoning into the reranker — semantic-ID encoding, three-stage training (mid-train + supervised reasoning + RL), and a reranker that uses the LLM's reasoning ability rather than just relevance scoring.

## Summary

Existing LLM-as-reranker work has three limitations the paper enumerates: most efforts focus on retrieval/ranking and overlook reranking; LLMs are typically used zero-shot or supervised-fine-tuned, not RL-tuned; and items are commonly represented by non-semantic IDs that do not scale industrially. GR2 addresses all three. It uses a tokenizer producing ≥99%-unique semantic IDs, mid-trains a pretrained LLM on those IDs, generates high-quality reasoning traces from a stronger LLM with prompting + rejection sampling for SFT, then RL-tunes for ranking quality. The setting is recommendation, but the recipe travels.

For a practitioner: even if you are not in recommendations, GR2 is one of the strongest examples in the kept set of a full training pipeline that gets reasoning capability into a reranker. The semantic-ID + three-stage-training pattern is replicable for other ranking tasks where you need the reranker to actually reason, not just score.

## Specifics

- Domain: recommendation systems (but recipe is general).
- Stage 1: semantic ID encoding (tokenizer with ≥99% uniqueness).
- Stage 2: mid-training the pretrained LLM on semantic IDs.
- Stage 3: SFT on high-quality reasoning traces (generated by stronger LLM with rejection sampling).
- Stage 4: RL fine-tune.
- Industry-team authorship — practical / production-relevant.
