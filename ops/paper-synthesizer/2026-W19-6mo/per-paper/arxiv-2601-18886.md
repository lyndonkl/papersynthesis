## Headline

XProvence fuses context pruning into the reranker for free — no extra forward pass — across 16 trained languages and 100+ supported, with weights released openly.

## Summary

Provence had introduced the idea of integrating zero-cost context pruning directly into the re-ranking model — pruning is a side-effect of reranking, not a separate stage. XProvence generalizes that work multilingually: trained on 16 languages, transferring to 100+, with minimal-to-no performance degradation across four multilingual QA benchmarks. Weights are publicly released.

For a practitioner: if you are running RAG in any non-English setting, XProvence is the cleanest piece of off-the-shelf infrastructure in the kept set. The "zero-cost" framing — pruning happens inside reranking with no extra latency — is the practitioner-relevant property: you are not adding a stage, you are getting pruning for free.

## Specifics

- Trained on 16 languages; supports 100+ via cross-lingual transfer.
- Architecture: pruning fused into the reranker (no extra forward pass).
- Benchmarks: four multilingual QA datasets.
- Result: minimal-to-no performance degradation while pruning context.
- Released: https://huggingface.co/naver/xprovence-reranker-bgem3-v2
