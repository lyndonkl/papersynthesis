## Headline

PruneRAG attacks two persistent failure modes of iterative RAG — evidence forgetting and uncontrolled query expansion — by building a confidence-guided query-decomposition tree that prunes uncertain branches and accepts confident answers early.

## Summary

Iterative RAG has two well-known failure modes: as reasoning chains deepen, retrieved evidence is forgotten by the generator, and uncontrolled query expansion produces redundant retrieval. PruneRAG operationalizes a fix: build a query-decomposition tree, expand it adaptively (regulating width and depth), use confidence scoring to accept reliable answers and prune uncertain branches, and use entity-level anchors for fine-grained retrieval. The paper also defines Evidence Forgetting Rate as a new metric for quantifying the first failure mode.

For a practitioner: this is a packaged answer to a real problem you will hit if you build an iterative RAG agent. The Evidence Forgetting Rate metric is itself useful — practitioners can adopt the metric without adopting the full PruneRAG architecture, just to know when their pipeline is dropping evidence under depth.

## Specifics

- Failure modes targeted: evidence forgetting, uncontrolled query expansion (inefficiency).
- Mechanisms: adaptive node expansion (width/depth control); confidence-guided accept/prune; entity-level anchors for fine-grained retrieval.
- New metric: Evidence Forgetting Rate (EFR).
- Useful even partially adopted — the EFR metric is portable.
