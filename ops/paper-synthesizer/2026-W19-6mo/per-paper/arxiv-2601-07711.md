## Headline

Enhanced RAG (workflow with dedicated modules) and Agentic RAG (LLM orchestrates) both outperform basic RAG, but neither is strictly better than the other — the choice depends on query complexity and cost tolerance, not on principle.

## Summary

The paper directly answers the question its title asks. It compares three RAG paradigms across multiple scenarios and dimensions: basic RAG (vanilla retrieve-then-generate), Enhanced RAG (workflow with dedicated modules for query rewrite, reranking, etc.), and Agentic RAG (LLM-orchestrated). The empirical conclusion is that neither Enhanced nor Agentic dominates strictly — each has scenarios where it is preferable, and basic RAG remains a viable default for simple queries.

For a practitioner: this is the most direct version of the "should I add agency or just enhancements?" question in the kept set, and the answer is honest — it depends. If your queries are simple and your latency budget is tight, basic RAG plus a reranker may be enough. If your queries require multi-step reasoning, agentic gives you the flexibility but at cost. The paper provides the trade-off data to actually decide.

## Specifics

- Practical framing: the paper exists to give practitioners the trade-off data, not to advocate.
