## Headline

Direct Corpus Interaction (DCI) lets the agent grep the raw corpus directly with general-purpose terminal tools — no embedder, no vector index, no retrieval API — and outperforms strong sparse, dense, AND reranking baselines on multiple benchmarks.

## Summary

The paper takes the "no embedder" position to its logical conclusion. Modern retrieval — lexical or semantic — exposes the corpus through a fixed similarity interface that compresses access to a single top-k step. For agentic search, this becomes a bottleneck: exact lexical constraints, sparse-clue conjunctions, local context checks, and multi-step hypothesis refinement are hard via a conventional retriever, and evidence filtered out early cannot be recovered. DCI gives the agent general-purpose terminal tools — grep, file reads, shell commands, lightweight scripts — and lets it search the raw corpus directly. No offline indexing; adapts naturally to evolving corpora. Across IR benchmarks (BEIR, BRIGHT) and end-to-end agentic search (BrowseComp-Plus), DCI outperforms strong sparse, dense, and reranking baselines.

For a practitioner: DCI is the strongest version in the kept set of the "rethink the retrieval interface" trajectory. The author list is unusually deep for an arXiv preprint, and the empirical claim is unusually strong — outperforming all three families. This is the paper to track over the next 2-3 months: if it replicates beyond the authors' setup, the operational implications for the vector-store paradigm are large. Pilot, do not commit to production yet.
