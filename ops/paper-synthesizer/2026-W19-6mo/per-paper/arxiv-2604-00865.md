## Headline

Doctor-RAG decouples failure diagnosis from repair: identify the earliest failure point in the reasoning trajectory, then intervene only there while reusing all validated reasoning prefixes — minimal-cost correction instead of pipeline re-runs.

## Summary

Agentic RAG, when reasoning trajectories grow long, fails frequently. The standard responses are diagnostic-only or rerun-the-whole-pipeline; both are wasteful. Doctor-RAG decomposes failure handling into two stages: (1) trajectory-level failure diagnosis with a coverage-gated taxonomy that identifies the earliest failure point; (2) tool-conditioned local repair that intervenes only at that point while reusing validated reasoning prefixes and retrieved evidence. The mechanism keeps cost minimal by separating attribution from correction.

For a practitioner: this is the right abstraction. Most of the engineering work in production iterative-RAG pipelines today is "did it fail; if so, run again from scratch." Doctor-RAG's prefix-reuse pattern is what production systems should aspire to. Even partial adoption — just the failure-diagnosis stage as a circuit breaker — pays off.

## Specifics

- Two stages: trajectory-level failure diagnosis (coverage-gated taxonomy); tool-conditioned local repair.
- Localization: earliest failure point in the reasoning trajectory.
- Reuse: validated reasoning prefixes + retrieved evidence preserved.
- Cost: minimal-cost intervention vs full pipeline rerun.
- Conceptually clean: separates attribution from correction.
