## Headline

In cold-start movie recommendation, LLM-based cross-encoder rerankers fail catastrophically against simple popularity baselines (HR@10: 0.268 vs 0.008) — the diagnostic locates the bottleneck at retrieval coverage, not reranker capacity.

## Summary

The paper is a controlled diagnostic of cross-encoder rerankers in cold-start movie recommendation, using the Serendipity-2018 dataset across 500 users and multiple seeds. Three failure modes are identified and quantified: low retrieval coverage at candidate generation (recall@200 = 0.109 vs 0.609 baseline), severe exposure bias (rerankers concentrating recommendations on 3 unique items vs 497 for random baseline), and minimal score discrimination (Cohen's d = 0.13). The paper then shows popularity-based ranking outperforms LLM reranking by ~30x on HR@10 (0.268 vs 0.008). The bottleneck is upstream — retrieval coverage — not reranker capacity.

For a practitioner: this paper is the corrective the field needed. Cross-encoders are powerful, but in any setting where retrieval coverage is poor (cold start being one example), rerankers cannot rescue you. The lesson is general — diagnose your retrieval coverage before attributing failures to the reranker.
