## Headline

Replacing the token-level "gather" phase in multivector retrieval with a learned-sparse retriever produces a smaller, more semantically coherent candidate set — collapsing multivector retrieval into the standard two-stage architecture without quality loss.

## Summary

Multivector retrievers (ColBERT-family) are powerful but expensive: the gather-and-refine pipeline does an exhaustive token-level pass to find candidates, then full multivector scoring to rerank. The paper's contribution is to swap out the token-level gather phase entirely, replacing it with a single-vector learned-sparse retriever. The result is a smaller, more semantically coherent candidate set and a pipeline that looks like the classic two-stage retrieval-then-rerank architecture instead of the bespoke gather-and-refine.

For a practitioner: if you are running ColBERT-family late-interaction in production and the token-level gather is a bottleneck, this paper is your migration path. The framing — "recast multivector retrieval as standard two-stage" — is also useful conceptually because it makes the pipeline easier to reason about and easier to swap pieces in and out of.
