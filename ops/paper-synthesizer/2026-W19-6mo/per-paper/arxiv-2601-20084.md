## Headline

IMRNNs add bidirectional inference-time modulation to any dense retriever — query-conditioned document embeddings and corpus-feedback-conditioned query embeddings — without the latency cost of a reranker.

## Summary

Dense retrievers are typically static: query and document embeddings are computed in isolation, which obscures the bidirectional semantic relationship and makes them hard to interpret. IMRNNs add two lightweight adapters at inference time: one conditions document embeddings on the current query; the other refines the query embedding using corpus-level feedback from initially retrieved documents. The modulation is iterative, exposing interpretable semantic dependencies without requiring a separate reranker forward pass.

For a practitioner: the headline story is interpretability, but the secondary story — getting some of the benefit of bidirectional query-document interaction at far lower latency than a reranker — may be the more relevant one for production. Worth piloting if you have a custom dense retriever you want to make adaptive without inserting a cross-encoder stage.

## Specifics

- Architecture: lightweight bidirectional adapters on top of any dense retriever.
- Two adapters: query-conditioned document modulation; corpus-feedback-conditioned query modulation.
- Iterative modulation at inference time.
- Drop-in: works with any dense retriever; no retraining of the base model required.
- Reported: improves both interpretability and retrieval performance.
