## Headline

Tool-augmented agents with only basic keyword search reach 90%+ of vector-RAG performance on a controlled benchmark — the headline that started the "do we need vectors?" thread that runs through the next five months.

## Summary

The paper does one thing and does it cleanly: it strips a tool-using agent down to having only keyword-search tools (no embeddings, no vector index) and measures the gap to a conventional vector-RAG baseline. The result the title states is the result the experiments back up: 90%+ of vector-RAG performance, with much simpler operational requirements. The implications cascade — if you do not need a vector index, you do not need an embedding model, you do not need an indexing pipeline, you do not need to re-index when documents update.

For a practitioner: this paper is the cleanest version of the "agent + simple search beats infrastructure-heavy RAG" argument. It does not claim vector-RAG is wrong; it claims the marginal value over agentic-keyword-search may not justify the operational cost in many settings. Worth piloting on your real corpus before committing to a vector-store deployment.
