## Headline

Two test-time-only modules — contextualization and de-duplication — added to Search-R1 yield +5.6% EM and -10.5% turns on multi-hop QA without retraining.

## Summary

The paper sits on top of Search-R1, a known iterative-RAG baseline. The contributions are two test-time modules: (1) contextualization, which integrates retrieved information into the reasoning prompt more effectively; (2) de-duplication, which replaces previously retrieved documents with the next most relevant ones to avoid redundancy. The combination is evaluated on HotpotQA and Natural Questions; the headline is +5.6% EM and -10.5% turns.

For a practitioner: this paper is the cleanest "free improvement" in the kept set for anyone using Search-R1 or a similar iterative-RAG baseline. No retraining, no architectural changes — just two test-time modules. The dedup module in particular is a generally useful pattern: if your iterative RAG retrieves the same document twice, the second retrieval is wasted compute and wasted context.

## Specifics

- Baseline: Search-R1 (Jin et al., 2025).
- Module 1: contextualization — better integration of retrieved evidence into the prompt.
- Module 2: de-duplication — replace already-retrieved docs with next-most-relevant.
- Datasets: HotpotQA, Natural Questions.
- Headline: +5.6% EM, -10.5% turns.
- Test-time only — no retraining required.
