## Headline

Vector-based agentic RAG with hybrid search and metadata filtering wins 68% of head-to-head comparisons against non-vector hierarchical-node systems on SEC filings, but the gap is smaller than the discourse suggests, and the cost story differs.

## Summary

The first systematic head-to-head between vector-based and non-vector RAG on SEC 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K filings (1,200 documents, 150-question benchmark). Two enhancements are layered onto the vector-based architecture: cross-encoder reranking and small-to-big chunk retrieval. The authors measure retrieval (MRR, Recall@5), answer quality (LLM-as-judge pairwise), latency, and preprocessing cost. The headline is a 68% win rate for vector-based agentic RAG with hybrid search + metadata filtering — but the framing is honest: hierarchical-node systems do not lose; they just lose more often, and they win on preprocessing cost.

For a practitioner: this paper anchors the entire 6-month thread of vector-vs-non-vector debate. If you are building a financial-domain RAG today, hybrid-search-with-metadata + cross-encoder reranking + small-to-big chunking is the empirically-supported default. The non-vector alternatives are real and worth piloting where preprocessing cost dominates.

## Specifics

- Benchmark: 1,200 SEC 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K filings; 150 questions.
- Vector-based agentic RAG with hybrid search + metadata filtering: 68% win rate vs hierarchical node-based.
- Two layered enhancements on the vector arm: cross-encoder reranking; small-to-big chunk retrieval.
- Latency: comparable across vector and node-based systems.
- Hidden cost: preprocessing — the vector arm's cost lives in indexing time.
