## Headline
When Q-learning agents condition on co-players' past actions and interact in groups (hypergraph hyperedges) rather than pairs, the Public Goods Game's cooperation curve no longer has one tipping point — it has two abrupt transitions separating absence-of-cooperation, medium-cooperation, and high-cooperation regions, with a time-averaged "chessboard" spatial pattern that promotes the first transition and suppresses the second.

## Summary
The authors define an "other-regarding reinforcement-learning evolutionary game" (OR-RLEG) in which each agent's Q-learning state encodes its own previous action plus the count of co-contributors in its hyperedge, and run it on a von Neumann hypergraph (each hyperedge = focal site + 4 lattice neighbours). Sweeping the synergy factor r̂, they report a robust three-region phase diagram with `r̂₁* ≈ 0.534` (AC→MC) and `r̂₂* ≈ 0.803` (MC→HC), in contrast to the single transition (~0.96) that the Fermi-rule imitation benchmark and self-regarding RL produce. The first transition is given an analytic treatment in the low-exploration limit as a crossover between a self-looped 00↔00 (mutual defection persists) mode and a cyclic 10↔01 (free-rider reciprocates a contributor's kindness next round) mode, yielding `r̂₁* ≈ (1 − ε/2) / [(1 − ε/2) + γ(1 − ε/2)^|e|]` (Eq. 18), which the authors describe as an "approximated" first transition point and validate by sweeping γ and ε. The second transition is identified only empirically through finite-size crossing in the curves' inset, and the chessboard mechanism for its opposite-signed effect is presented as a "plausible conjecture" grounded in Cohen's-κ mode analysis of high- vs low-cooperation hyperedges, not a derivation.

## Specifics

- **Model setup.** Agents sit on an `L × L` periodic lattice; each hyperedge groups a focal agent with its four von Neumann neighbours (k = 5 players per hyperedge). State `s = (n_c, a)` encodes the agent's own previous action `a ∈ {0, 1}` and the previous count `n_c` of *other* contributors in its hyperedge. Q-learning parameters: α (learning rate), γ (discount), ε (exploration). The synergy factor `r̂ = r / |e|` is the control parameter.
- **Initiator vs participant asymmetry (load-bearing modelling choice).** Within hyperedge `eⁱ`, agent `i` updates its Q-table after the game; the four neighbours act but do not update from this game (they update only when they are themselves initiators in their own hyperedge). No ablation against a symmetric-update variant is reported.
- **Three regions, two transitions.** AC (`f̄_c ≈ 0`), MC (`f̄_c ≈ 0.65–0.85`), HC (`f̄_c ≳ 0.96`), separated by `r̂₁* ≈ 0.534` and `r̂₂* ≈ 0.803` (Fig. 2). The phase structure is "robust" across `|N| ∈ {9², 10², 19², 20², 29², 30²}`; OR-RLEG reaches full cooperation by `r̂ ≈ 0.85` versus `r̂ ≈ 1.0` for the Fermi-rule benchmark.
- **First-transition mechanism (analytical, ε ≪ 1).** Eq. 18: `r̂₁* ≈ (1 − ε/2) / [(1 − ε/2) + γ(1 − ε/2)^|e|]`. Long-sightedness (high γ) lowers `r̂₁*`; higher exploration (high ε) raises it. Authors describe the mechanism as the cyclic 10↔01 mode "more likely to reciprocate kindness" overtaking the 00↔00 self-loop — interpretive overlay on a competing-modes calculation.
- **Validation range.** Fig. 7 sweeps γ ∈ {0.5, 0.7, 0.9} and ε ∈ {0.010, 0.015, 0.020}. The ε range is narrow (factor of 2); whether the approximation breaks down at moderate exploration (ε = 0.05–0.1) is not addressed.
- **Second transition is empirical only.** `r̂₂* ≈ 0.803` is read off the finite-size crossing point of curves for odd vs even `|N|` (Fig. 2 inset), invoking finite-size-scaling theory (Privman, ref. [59]); no analytic counterpart to Eq. 18 is given. Authors flag this as future work.
- **Chessboard pattern (load-bearing for the spatial claim).** In the MC region, time-averaged local cooperation `f̂_c^i` alternates high–low–high–low across neighbouring hyperedges (Fig. 3c, r̂ = 0.72); contributor preference `p̂_c^i` is spatially anti-correlated with hyperedge cooperation (initiators in low-coop hyperedges *prefer* to contribute). The pattern is absent in single-step snapshots (Fig. B.11) and emerges only through time accumulation — described as a "novel spatial phenomenon" relative to prior PGG-RL work where snapshot chessboards were reported.
- **Chessboard's opposite-signed roles.** The regular symmetry breaking is argued to promote AC→MC but suppress MC→HC. Evidence is correlational: odd/even-`|N|` finite-size crossing in Fig. 2 inset, plus Case I (r̂ = 0.72) vs Case II (r̂ = 0.84) Cohen's-κ matrices showing cyclic modes destabilising as r̂ rises (Figs. 8–10). Authors hedge this as a "plausible conjecture"; no controlled intervention that suppresses or enforces chessboard structure independently is reported.
- **Transition character — tension within the paper.** The abstract calls both transitions "abrupt", but Sec. IV A 2 describes `r̂₁*` as "continuous, demonstrating that the emergence of cooperation occurs gradually rather than abruptly." The visual sharpness in Fig. 2 is finite-size-influenced; readers should treat the abruptness framing with care.
- **Hysteresis is not tested.** For a system the authors call "two abrupt transitions", no analysis is reported of whether lowering r̂ from the HC regime recovers the same `r̂₁*` and `r̂₂*` or whether defection cascades back at different (lower) thresholds.
- **Cohen's-κ usage.** The temporal correlation coefficient `κ(s, s') = [f̄(s, s') − f̄(s)f̄(s')] / [1 − f̄(s)f̄(s')]` is borrowed from the inter-rater-agreement statistic and used to read off self-loop modes (positive diagonal) vs cyclic modes (positive symmetric off-diagonals). The construction is presented without first-principles justification for why it captures temporal mode structure.

## Why this matters in this run's context

Cooperation-regime case in Cluster 5: shows that Q-learning agents conditioning on co-players' past actions in hyperedge groups produce *two* abrupt transitions rather than one, with the first derived in closed form — the orchestrator analog is that group-coordination structures often have multiple regime boundaries rather than a single "cooperates / doesn't" tipping point, and the first boundary can be located analytically from the discount factor and exploration rate.
