## Headline

In larval zebrafish, vsx2+ reticulospinal neurons are organized as a hub-and-spoke command hierarchy: rostral "Start" and "Steering" populations in rhombomeres r6-r7 feed a caudal-medullary r8 hub that aggregates inputs, builds activity through local recurrence, and issues the forward-swim command — a modular yet integrated architecture demonstrated by patterned optogenetic dissociation.

## Summary

Using an all-optical approach — volumetric calcium imaging plus patterned visible-light and two-photon holographic optogenetics plus single-cell anatomical reconstruction — the authors argue that descending motor commands in the zebrafish brainstem are not produced by a uniform pool but by anatomically segregated, hierarchically arranged modules. Focal columnar stimulation of vsx2+ neurons cleanly separates two programs: r5-r7 stimulation produces short-latency ipsilateral steering, while r8 stimulation produces longer-latency bilateral rhythmic forward swimming, with 90% of forward events (129/142) traced to r8 across 5 fish and 24 sites. The authors are explicit that columnar stimulation activates 4-6 neurons (up to 15 in r6) simultaneously, so the dissociation is established at the *cluster* level rather than the single-neuron level — they note "we cannot yet determine precisely which rostral vsx2+ RSNs in r6 trigger forward locomotion." Single-cell two-photon activation of 17/74 responsive cells failed in every case to recruit network-wide activity, which the authors read as evidence that single-neuron activity is *not sufficient* to initiate locomotion and that the r8 hub depends on recurrent dynamics.

## Specifics

- **Architecture (the load-bearing claim):** rostral r6-r7 vsx2+ "Start" cluster (active ~300 ms before bout onset across forward, left-turn, right-turn bouts) plus a r5-r7 steering cluster feed a caudal r8 + rostral-spinal forward hub. The hub contains local recurrent excitation (10/19 medullary lateral vsx2+ neurons have bifurcating axons; ascending + descending branches) and receives ascending V2a CiD feedback from spinal premotor circuits. Single-cell pcDronpa2 reconstructions (n=40) show rostral Start cells send descending, midline-crossing axon collaterals onto contralateral vsx2+ medullary neurons in r7-r8 and onto V2a spinal interneurons.

- **Latency hierarchy (the dynamics signature):** r8 forward latency 53.3 ± 20.2 ms vs r6 steering latency 6.7 ± 5.5 ms vs r7 steering 10.0 ± 7.9 ms (Kruskal-Wallis + pairwise Mann-Whitney, forward vs steering within r7 and r8 both p < 0.001). The authors interpret the ≥60 ms forward latency as activity build-up time in the recurrent r8 network — direct steering projections are fast, hub-mediated forward commands are slow.

- **Kinematic dissociation:** forward bouts show bilateral symmetric oscillations at 18.7 ± 2.8 Hz tail-beat frequency; steering produces single ipsilateral tail bends at 3.4 ± 2.8 Hz; turn bouts at 11.6 ± 4.0 Hz (all p < 0.001). Rostral stimulation produces larger amplitudes than caudal (r6 turn: 80.8 ± 25.0°; r8 turn: 32.4 ± 17.8°; p < 0.001) — a topographic amplitude gradient overlaid on the rostro-caudal program gradient.

- **"Start" identification method:** LASSO regression with 10-fold cross-validation on volumetric SCAPE calcium imaging (5.55 Hz) identified 207 top-weight candidate "Start" neurons across n=4 fish (reanalysis of ref 39 dataset). The authors invoke a Markov-blanket interpretation explicitly — high-weight LASSO predictors are *candidates* for the causal-inference Markov blanket of the behavior — and are clear this is a regression-derived candidate set, not a causal claim.

- **Causal asymmetry — single cell is not enough:** two-photon holographic single-cell activation of 17/74 responsive vsx2+ RSNs (across r5-r8) recruited zero network-wide locomotor episodes, while spontaneous fictive swims robustly activated the same population. The r8 single-cell stimulation failed entirely (0/17 cells responsive), which the authors attribute to opsin sensitivity rather than r8 unresponsiveness in principle — meaning the "single r8 cell insufficient" claim is inferred from r5-r7 negatives plus r8 columnar positives, not directly tested.

- **Hedging audit (preserved verbatim):** "putative 'Start' neurons" used throughout; the closing functional claim is "*can allow* rapid transitions" rather than "produces" or "enables." The title's strong wording ("reveals a hierarchical organization") is supported in the modular sense and in the latency-gradient + anatomical sense, but a directional pairing-perturbation experiment (activate rostral while silencing r8, or vice versa) is not performed — the hierarchical-directionality claim rests on morphology and latency, not on causal directionality.

- **Limits the operator should hold in mind:**
  - Columnar precision of ~4-6 neurons (up to 15 in r6) is the resolution floor; dissociation is at the rhombomere/cluster level, not at the cell-type level.
  - Calcium imaging at 5-6 Hz cannot distinguish "Start" cells from "maintain" cells; the "~300 ms before movement" timing is the upstream-cluster *recruitment window*, not a measured rostral-to-r8 propagation delay. The measured propagation-like signal is the ≥60 ms r8 hub build-up under optogenetic activation.
  - Bilateral output from unilateral r8 stimulation has two candidate mechanisms (contralateral vsx2+ hindbrain axons vs downstream V0v-derived MCoD commissural interneurons) and the paper leaves both open.
  - Cross-species mapping in the Discussion (rostral r6-r7 ≈ lamprey MRRN; r8 ≈ lamprey PRRN; analogy to mouse rostral Gi vsx2+) is interpretive homology, not data from this paper.

- **Why this paper is interesting outside neurobiology:** the architecture it instantiates is a layered command-relay system — upstream specialist populations (one for initiation, one for direction) feeding a downstream aggregator with internal recurrence and ascending feedback from the executor layer. The dissociation is *modular* (clean rostro-caudal program segregation) yet *integrated* (the caudal "Start" sub-cluster fires during *both* forward and turn bouts, so the r8 hub engages whenever forward propulsion is part of the motor program — turning is forward + steering, not steering alone). The latency hierarchy (10-15 ms direct steering vs ≥60 ms hub-mediated forward) is the temporal signature of priority-of-control across a hierarchical control system.
