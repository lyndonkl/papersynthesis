## Headline

Whether a system of locally interacting parts can settle into long-range order is determined by the topology of its interaction graph alone — clique-and-superclique hierarchies admit an ordered phase, one-dimensional windowed graphs (including simple decoder-only transformers under causal masking) do not.

## Summary

The paper generalizes the classical Peierls / Landau–Lifshitz domain-wall free-energy argument from one-dimensional spin chains to arbitrary planar graph Hamiltonians, proving a topological-equivalence theorem: all local Hamiltonians on graphs with the same combinatorial structure have asymptotically equivalent free energies, so the *capacity* for an ordered phase on a graph G is equivalent to that of a nearest-neighbour Ising model on G (Theorem 1 + Corollary 1, via the Bogoliubov inequality). From this the authors derive a necessary scaling condition for spontaneous order — domain-wall entropy and energy must scale at the same order in perimeter — and apply it uniformly to one-dimensional Potts chains, autoregressive models, and causally-masked decoder-only attention to conclude that none of these can sustain long-range order, while hierarchical clique-and-superclique graphs admit a non-empty critical-temperature window in which local cliques can be ordered even when the global system is not. The bridge to large language models is framed as an "explanation" of empirical long-context limitations for a "simple version" of an LLM, hedged explicitly because multi-head attention "is more sophisticated"; the analogy to schizophrenia-like derailment is offered as something the LLM failure mode "resembles," not as a derived equivalence.

## Specifics

- **Theorem 1 (topological equivalence)** — all local Hamiltonians on lattices with the same combinatorial structure have *asymptotically equivalent* free energies. Proven via the Bogoliubov inequality plus Lemmas 1–2. This is the load-bearing result; the rest of the paper applies it.
- **Corollary 1** — the capacity for self-organization on any graph G (existence or non-existence of a phase transition) reduces to that of a nearest-neighbour Ising on G. Topology, not interaction strength or stored-pattern count, is the relevant lever.
- **Proposition 1** — an ordered phase exists if domain-wall entropy and energy scale at the same order in perimeter (`O(f_S) = O(f_E)`). The negative results lean on the converse direction; the paper's framing as a "necessary" condition elides the necessary-vs-sufficient distinction and should be read with that elision in mind (abstract-only readers especially).
- **Theorem 2 (1D Potts)** and **Theorem 3 + Corollary 2 (autoregressive models)** — at any finite β, a 1D local Potts Hamiltonian with multiple stored patterns favors domain-wall formation, and an autoregressive AR(ω) model "is *unable to converge to a single stored pattern*."
- **Proposition 2 (decoder-only transformers)** — causally-masked attention "has no ordered phase," derived by embedding the modern-Hopfield-as-attention equivalence (Ramsauer et al. 2021) into the AR(ω) local-Hamiltonian framework. The result applies to a single-head, identity-weight attention map; the authors explicitly hedge that multi-head attention "is more sophisticated" and do not claim the result extends to real multi-head transformers. The LLM bridge is offered as an explanation of empirical phenomena reported in Kwa et al. 2025 (arXiv:2503.14499), not as a derivation about transformers in general.
- **Theorem 4 + Proposition 3 (hierarchical cliques)** — graphs built from cliques and supercliques admit a non-empty critical-temperature range whenever `ℓ/r > n_max^{n_max}/e` (with `n_max` the largest clique size), inside which individual cliques can be uniformly magnetized while the global system is not. This is the topological feature the authors offer as the formal account of why multiscale biological tissues, organs, and organisms can sustain partial coherence that 1D-windowed systems cannot.
- **Load-bearing assumptions worth carrying forward:** (i) *adiabatic approximation on edges* — the graph is fixed in time; the result does not address whether it survives edge creation/destruction, which is exactly what biological morphogenesis involves; (ii) *equilibrium assumption* — "throughout we have assumed that the free energy is minimised," with non-equilibrium / stochastic-thermodynamics extensions flagged as future work; (iii) the schizophrenia-derailment analogy in the conclusion is offered as "resembles" with quote marks around "hallucinations" — suggestive, not derived, and should be preserved with hedging intact in any downstream use.
- **Figures** carry no quantitative data (no plots, no error bars — purely theoretical). Figures 1–3, 5–7 are schematic illustrations of the definitions and the domain-wall and clique-hierarchy constructions; Figures 4 and 8 are conceptual cartoons motivating the biology–LLM analogy and do not establish it.
- **Systems-thinking handle for this run:** the paper names the *interaction-graph topology* — specifically, the presence of clique-and-superclique hierarchy — as the leverage point that determines whether a locally interacting collective can hold coherence across scales. The relevant primitive is the modular hierarchy with rich within-module coupling and sparser between-module coupling, not the strength or count of individual interactions.
