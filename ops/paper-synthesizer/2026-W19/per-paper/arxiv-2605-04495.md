# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Reranks RAG documents by how much they raise the generator's answer confidence, with a query-level gate that avoids interfering on already-confident queries.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
CAR (Confidence-Aware Reranking) shares the LCR / RRPO premise — relevance is not utility — but uses a different mechanism: estimate confidence via semantic consistency across multiple sampled answers under query-only vs query+document conditions, promote confidence-raising documents and demote confidence-lowering ones, preserve baseline order on already-confident queries. On four BEIR datasets, CAR improves NDCG@5 across sparse and dense retrievers, LLM-based and supervised rerankers, and four LLM backbones, with a notable 25.4% average lift to the YesNo reranker under Contriever, and Spearman ρ=0.964 between the ranking gain and downstream F1.
