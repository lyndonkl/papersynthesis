## Headline
AgenticRAGTracer is a 1,305-item benchmark with automated step-by-step trajectory validation that diagnoses *where* agentic RAG systems fail mid-reasoning, and reports GPT-5 at only 22.6% exact-match accuracy on its hardest 4-hop Inference split.

## Summary
The paper argues that existing multi-hop RAG benchmarks (HotpotQA, 2WikiMultihopQA, MuSiQue, BamboogleQA) score only final answers and so cannot tell whether an agentic-RAG system actually composed evidence across hops or shortcut through a single retrieval, and that prior LLM-generated alternatives (MoreHopQA, MultiHop-RAG, MINTQA) lack fine-grained quality control to serve as reliable evaluation substrates. To remedy this, the authors build AgenticRAGTracer through a six-step automated pipeline (Wikipedia filtering with anti-overlap against prior benchmarks → atomic QA generation → difficulty-bucketed filtering → multi-hop merging into Sequential/Inference and Parallel/Comparison topologies → three-stage logical-fidelity verification → SPARQL disambiguation plus three-annotator human review at Fleiss' κ = 0.65), then evaluate 13 LLMs under a unified Qwen-Agent / ReAct setup. The headline finding is that GPT-5 drops from 76.2 EM on 2-hop Comparison to 22.6 EM on 4-hop Inference, and cross-hop step-count diagnostics (Steps-C ≈ target hop, Steps-I drifts) are presented as evidence that failures are *predominantly characterized by* distorted reasoning chains rooted in a brittle initial task decomposition — not isolated tool-use errors.

## Specifics
- **Benchmark artifact.** 1,305 items across 11 domains, split by topology (Inference 651 / Comparison 717 — small asymmetry in the donut totals) and hop depth (2/3/4-hop). The 4-hop bucket carrying the headline number is small: ~128 items total, ~64 Inference.
- **Construction pipeline (Figure 1).** Six steps; the load-bearing filters are the three logical-fidelity criteria in §3.3 — *retrieval necessity* (not answerable from parametric knowledge), *dependency irreducibility* (breaks if any supporting doc is removed), and *grounded solvability* (solvable given the full evidence set). The merging prompts (Appendix C, Figures 5–10) encode "no intermediate-answer leakage, no spurious linkage, no trivial concatenation" operationally.
- **Two topologies.** Sequential/Inference (hop k's answer is needed to formulate hop k+1's query) and Parallel/Comparison (independent gathering of facts about multiple entities, then synthesis along a shared dimension). The paper claims the split exposes capabilities masked by single-axis multi-hop benchmarks (topology claim).
- **Headline numbers (Table 1, EM).** GPT-5 4-hop Inference 22.6, 4-hop Comparison 26.6; o4-mini 4-hop Inference 21.4, 4-hop Comparison 35.9; Grok-4 4-hop Inference 20.2; Qwen2.5-72B 4-hop Inference 6.0; DeepSeek-V3 4-hop Inference 2.4. Degradation with hop count is near-monotonic across nearly all models; Inference is harder than Comparison at matched hop count; the closed-vs-open gap is large. Single-run-per-model is the implicit reporting assumption — no seed variance, no error bars.
- **Diagnostic claim (Table 2).** MaxD / Steps-C / Steps-I diagnostics. Steps-I > Steps-C in almost every cell (e.g., GPT-5 4-hop Inference: Steps-C 5.85 vs Steps-I 8.25; o4-mini: 4.72 vs 7.78), which the authors read as quantitative evidence for the "distorted reasoning chains" claim — correct trajectories cluster near the target hop count, incorrect ones drift longer. DeepSeek-R1 4-hop Inference inverts this (4.17 / 2.76); the inversion is unremarked.
- **GPT-4o anomaly explained correlationally (Figure 3).** GPT-4o sits at low top-k (1.24–1.93 average) while top-tier models cluster around 4–5; the authors argue this conservative retrieval strategy starves the chain of evidence and accounts for GPT-4o's 15.6 EM on 4-hop Comparison vs o4-mini's 35.9. No controlled top-k intervention; the argument is correlational.
- **Error-mode evidence (Figure 4).** A single 4-hop UCLA-Bruins error case shows the model committing to a literal phrase as a search term in step 1 and then introducing a spurious subgoal, *consistent with* the §4.4 narrative that initial decomposition determines trajectory viability. Existence proof, not a frequency estimate.
- **Hedging in the paper's own language.** §4.4 mixes hedged phrasing ("failures are predominantly characterized by," "highlights an inability") with stronger causal language ("cascading failures fundamentally rooted in a breakdown of initial task decomposition"). The abstract states results without softening ("GPT-5 achieves only 22.6%"); preserve both registers rather than promoting or demoting.
- **Primacy claim.** "First Agentic RAG benchmark automatically constructed with step-by-step validation" — defensible but qualified to a specific definitional combination, since MoreHopQA / MultiHop-RAG / MINTQA already do LLM-generated multi-hop construction; primacy hinges on what counts as step-by-step validation.
- **Quality / authenticity claim.** Inter-annotator Fleiss' κ = 0.65 (substantial agreement); claims no overlap with HotpotQA / 2Wiki / MuSiQue / BamboogleQA. Authenticity case studies (Appendix B) are asymmetric — positive examples from this pipeline, negative examples from prior benchmarks, no symmetric quantitative comparison.
- **Reproducibility surface.** Uses FlashRAG corpus + index for index-comparable evaluation; code/data at `https://github.com/YqjMartin/AgenticRAGTracer` (not verified). ReAct under Qwen-Agent unifies the 13-model setup; EM, F1, and LLM-as-Judge (GPT-4o-mini at temperature 0) are the reported metrics.
- **Relevance for retrieval-for-agents.** The benchmark targets the diagnostic gap between outcome-only multi-hop QA and *trajectory-level* evaluation for agentic RAG — i.e., it speaks directly to whether iterative / multi-step retrieval primitives in an agent loop actually compose, and where they break. Sits adjacent to the RL-trained agentic-RAG checkpoints (Search-R1, ReSearch, R-Search) it evaluates.
