# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Standardizes the comparison: under agentic search, dense RAG nearly catches up to GraphRAG, but GraphRAG retains an advantage on complex multi-hop reasoning when its offline cost can amortize.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
RAGSearch is a unified benchmark that evaluates dense RAG and representative GraphRAG methods as retrieval infrastructures under agentic search, covering training-free + training-based agentic inference across multiple QA benchmarks. The paper standardizes LLM backbones, retrieval budgets, and inference protocols; reports answer accuracy, offline preprocessing cost, online inference efficiency, and stability on full test sets. The headline finding: agentic search substantially improves dense RAG and narrows the GraphRAG gap (especially in RL-based settings); GraphRAG remains advantageous for complex multi-hop reasoning, with more stable agentic search behavior when its offline cost is amortized.
