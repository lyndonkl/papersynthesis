## Headline
Agentic search narrows the dense-vs-GraphRAG gap on general QA but explicit graph structure still appears advantageous for complex multi-hop reasoning, at a non-trivial offline-construction cost.

## Summary
The paper releases `RAGSearch`, a benchmark that holds the agentic control layer fixed (a ReAct-style think-search-information-answer loop) while swapping the retrieval backend across dense RAG and five GraphRAG variants (GraphRAG, RAPTOR, HippoRAG2, HyperGraphRAG, LinearRAG), and evaluates the grid under three regimes — single-shot, training-free agentic (Search-o1, GraphSearch), and RL-trained agentic (Search-R1, Graph-R1 with GRPO) — across six QA datasets split between general (NQ, PopQA, TriviaQA) and multi-hop (HotpotQA, 2Wiki, Musique). The authors argue that agentic interaction supplies *implicit* structure that compensates for missing graph structure on simple/general QA, but *explicit* graph structure remains "crucial for robust multi-hop reasoning"; on multi-hop the GraphRAG–Dense Contain-EM gap shrinks from +27.23 (single-shot) to +26.59 (under GraphSearch), which the authors frame as a 32.3% relative reduction and verbally describe as "partially mitigated" rather than closed. Entity-centric HippoRAG2 holds the strongest backend position on multi-hop under both training-free and RL agentic regimes, and Table 8 quantifies the cost GraphRAG buys (e.g., GraphRAG itself at 1.72h construction, $13.19/1M tokens, 22,160 context tokens per query versus dense RAG's near-zero offline construction).

## Specifics

- **Benchmark released — `RAGSearch`.** A unified evaluation harness that decouples the agentic control layer (ReAct-style think → search → information → answer loop) from the retrieval backend, holding the agent and inference budget fixed while varying the backend. The grid is three regimes (single-shot / training-free agentic / RL-trained agentic) × six retrieval backends × six QA datasets.
- **Backends benchmarked.** Dense RAG plus five representative GraphRAGs spanning four index structures: GraphRAG (community-summary tree), RAPTOR (recursive abstractive tree), HippoRAG2 (entity-centric graph with Personalized PageRank–style propagation), HyperGraphRAG (hyperedges over n-ary facts), and LinearRAG (relation-free Tri-Graph from entity extraction + semantic linking).
- **Agentic systems instantiated.** Search-o1 (training-free, reasoning-driven on-demand search with a Reason-in-Documents compression module), GraphSearch (training-free, decomposition → search → verification → answer multi-agent workflow), Search-R1 (RL-trained dense-RAG agent), Graph-R1 (RL-trained GraphRAG agent). RL training uses GRPO with KL regularization to a reference policy.
- **Datasets and metric.** General QA: NQ, PopQA, TriviaQA. Multi-hop: HotpotQA, 2Wiki, Musique. Primary metric is Contain Exact Match (substring containment of the normalized answer); F1 reported as confirmation.
- **Headline numerical result (Table 1, Contain-EM).** Under single-shot inference, the best GraphRAG variant beats dense RAG by roughly +27 points on multi-hop QA (+27.70 HotpotQA, +27.03 2Wiki, +26.99 Musique) but by only +0.47 on general QA on average. Under training-free agentic search (GraphSearch), dense RAG itself jumps substantially on multi-hop (HotpotQA 33.76 → 38.22), yet GraphSearch + GraphRAG still leads on multi-hop (HotpotQA 58.64 vs 38.22 = +20.42).
- **The gap-closure claim, with the authors' own framing.** §5.2.2 reports the multi-hop Dense–GraphRAG gap "decreases from +27.23 (single-shot) to +26.59" — an absolute reduction of 0.64 points, which the paper frames as a 32.3% reduction relative to the second-best GraphRAG variant. The denominator for the 32.3% is not derived in-line; the authors' verbal claim is that agentic search "partially mitigates" the absence of explicit structure rather than eliminating the gap.
- **Best backend on multi-hop is consistent.** HippoRAG2 (entity-centric) achieves Avg Rank 1.00 under Search-o1 and 1.33 under GraphSearch on multi-hop; dense RAG sits around Avg Rank 5–6 on multi-hop in both regimes. On General QA, dense RAG is competitive (Avg Rank 2.33 under Search-o1).
- **Robustness signal (Table 3).** GraphRAG yields lower Contain-EM variance and higher recall under iterative agentic search (e.g., HippoRAG2 variance 0.22 on HotpotQA vs 1.03 for dense), and Graph-R1-HippoRAG2 uses fewer search turns (1.71 on HotpotQA vs 1.82 for Search-R1). Per-cell sample size and seed count are not stated; the robustness claim rests on this two-dataset, two-pair slice.
- **RL-paradigm finding (RQ5).** GRPO outperforms PPO and RPP on Graph-R1 across HotpotQA / NQ / Musique / 2Wiki for HypergraphRAG-3B, LinearRAG-3B, and HippoRAG2-3B backbones (Figure 4). Number of seeds is not reported.
- **Backbone-scale finding (Tables 4–5).** Larger backbones partially narrow the Graph–Dense gap: the average Search-o1 Dense–GraphRAG gap is reported to drop from 14.70 at 7B to 9.75 at 32B. The 32B comparison is run only in the training-free Search-o1 regime, not for RL-based methods.
- **Cost side of the trade-off (Table 8, on NQ).** GraphRAG: 1.72h construction, $13.19/1M tokens, 22,160 context tokens per query. RAPTOR: 1.70h, $6.38, 8.4s/query retrieval latency. HippoRAG2: 1.19h, $2.85, 3,229 ctx tokens. LinearRAG: 0.68h, $0, 4,600 ctx tokens. Dense RAG's offline construction is implicitly zero; its online retrieval cost is not tabulated in the same table.
- **Headline claim, in the authors' words.** Explicit graph structure remains "crucial for robust multi-hop reasoning" even under agentic search; agentic interaction supplies implicit structure that compensates for missing graph structure on simple/general QA but does not close the multi-hop gap. The authors also report that "well-designed training-free workflows remain highly competitive and can even exceed RL-based systems" — Graph-R1 trails some GraphSearch + graph configurations on multi-hop.
- **Caveats carried forward from the paper.** (i) The 32.3% relative-reduction figure rests on an unstated denominator. (ii) Confidence intervals are reported only in Table 3, for two datasets and two system pairs; Tables 1–2 are point estimates without seeds. (iii) All experiments use Qwen2.5 variants — cross-family generalization is untested. (iv) Search-o1's web-search backend is replaced with a Wikipedia retrieval server "formatted to mimic web search results," so the Search-o1 numbers here are not directly comparable to Search-o1's published results. (v) The arXiv manuscript still carries unfilled ACM template placeholders ("Trovato et al.," "Do Not Use This Code → Generate the Correct Terms for Your Paper") — cosmetic, but it signals the posting was not fully cleaned.
