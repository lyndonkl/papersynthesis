## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Diagnostic 3x3 study of agent-memory write strategies vs retrieval methods finds that retrieval method dominates (20-pt accuracy spread) while write strategy contributes only 3-8 points — and raw chunked storage matches summarization at zero LLM-call cost.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
This work crosses three write strategies (raw chunks, Mem0-style fact extraction, MemGPT-style summarization) with three retrieval methods (cosine, BM25, hybrid reranking) on LoCoMo. The empirical claim is sharp: retrieval method swings accuracy across a 20-pt range (57.1% to 77.2%); write strategy across only 3-8 points. Failure analysis shows breakdowns concentrate at retrieval, not utilization. The paper argues that under current retrieval practices, improving retrieval quality yields larger gains than write-time sophistication — and raw chunks may be sufficient.
