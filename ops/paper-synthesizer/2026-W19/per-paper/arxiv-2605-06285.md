# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Runs the agentic-RAG reasoning + retrieval loop in continuous latent space rather than via token-by-token natural-language generation, cutting inference latency by approximately 90% at comparable accuracy.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
LatentRAG argues the latency bottleneck of agentic RAG is the autoregressive generation of long thoughts and subqueries. Its solution shifts both reasoning and retrieval into latent space: latent thoughts and latent subquery tokens are produced directly from hidden states in a single forward pass; the LLM is aligned with a dense retriever in the latent space; a parallel decoder translates latent tokens back to text for transparency. On seven benchmarks, LatentRAG achieves performance comparable to explicit agentic RAG while reducing inference latency approximately 90%, narrowing the gap to traditional single-step RAG.
