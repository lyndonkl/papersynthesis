# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Trains no model: scores documents by an LLM's own confidence (Maximum Semantic Cluster Proportion of multinomial samples), and reports up to +20.6% NDCG@5 on BEIR/TREC over fine-tuned rerankers using only 7-9B-parameter open LLMs.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
LCR (LLM-Confidence Reranker) argues fine-tuned rerankers throw away the LLM's own latent confidence signal. Its mechanism is a two-stage process: assess document confidence via multinomial sampling and clustering (MSCP); then bin and multi-level sort, preserving original ranking on already-confident queries (a noise-protective gate). On BEIR + TREC, LCR with BM25 and Contriever retrievers consistently improves NDCG@5 by up to 20.6%, with ablations claimed to validate that LLM confidence positively correlates with document relevance.
