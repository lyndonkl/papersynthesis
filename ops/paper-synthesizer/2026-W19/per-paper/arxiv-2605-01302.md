## Headline
CoRM-RAG retrains a lightweight cross-encoder reranker to score documents by whether they steer the LLM to the correct answer under simulated user-bias rewrites of the query, rather than by semantic similarity to the query as written.

## Summary
The paper argues that maximizing semantic relevance is the wrong retrieval objective whenever the query carries cognitive bias (false premise, confirmation bias, topical distraction), because the closest-matching passages are exactly the sycophantic distractors that reinforce the LLM's hallucination — a failure mode the authors name the "Relevance-Robustness Gap." Their fix is a two-phase pipeline: a Cognitive Perturbation Protocol uses an Adversary LLM to rewrite each training query into three bias types and a Teacher LLM scores how reliably each candidate document still yields the gold answer under perturbation, producing soft robustness labels that distill into an Evidence Critic (a DeBERTa-v3-large cross-encoder) trained with a hybrid listwise-ranking-plus-pointwise-BCE loss. On the authors' own Biased-NQ test set and on TruthfulQA, the Critic is reported to "significantly outperform" strong dense retrievers and an LLM reranker at ~215 ms latency, and its calibrated sigmoid is used as a robustness score for risk-aware abstention.

## Specifics
- **Named system and components.** CoRM-RAG (Counterfactual Risk Minimization for RAG); Cognitive Perturbation Protocol (Phase 1, training-time); Evidence Critic (Phase 2, inference-time lightweight cross-encoder). The retrieval target is redefined as a "Robustness Utility" `U(d,x) = E[1(M(x⊕δ, d) = y)]` over a distribution of cognitive perturbations δ.
- **Bias taxonomy used in perturbations.** Three types: False Premise, Confirmation Bias, Topical Distraction. Adversary LLM is Qwen3-32B at training and GPT-4o at test, chosen this way to limit train/test leakage on the perturbations themselves.
- **Critic training.** DeBERTa-v3-large cross-encoder distilled on ~50k (query, perturbation) listwise groups derived from ~10k unique NQ queries × 5 perturbations each. Loss combines a temperature-scaled listwise ranking loss (`L_rank`, Eq. 5, τ=1.0 chosen) with pointwise binary cross-entropy (`L_conf`, Eq. 6) so the sigmoid output is usable as a calibrated abstention threshold.
- **Headline numbers (single-run point estimates; no error bars, no significance tests despite "significantly outperforms" language).** On Biased-NQ (3,610 perturbed NQ queries, Qwen3-8B generator): CoRM-RAG 52.6% vs PA-CE (same-data-augmented cross-encoder baseline) 48.0% vs stock MS-MARCO Cross-Encoder 40.9% vs GPT-4o LLM-Rerank 51.0%. Latency 215 ms vs ~20 s for the LLM reranker. The paper's "Robustness Gap" (clean − biased accuracy) is 1.3 for CoRM-RAG, lowest in Table 1.
- **Retrieval-quality detail.** On the 2,728-query subset where Contriever's top-100 contains a gold passage, CoRM-RAG reports +11.8% Recall@1 over the Cross-Encoder (36.5% → 48.3%); a per-query scatter shows CoRM-RAG outranks the Cross-Encoder on 45.7% of queries vs 27.1% the other way (net +18.6%).
- **Risk-aware abstention.** Risk-coverage curve on Biased-NQ rises from 52.6% accuracy at 100% coverage to 78.0% at 50% coverage; the authors flag residual jaggedness at <10% coverage as "fatal hallucinations" the Critic remains overconfident on.
- **Cross-generator transfer.** The Qwen-trained Critic is reported to preserve gains when paired with Llama-3-8B (Biased-NQ 46.5% vs 42.8% for Cross-Encoder baseline) and GPT-4o as the generator. The extraction flags that Teacher (Qwen3-14B) and Qwen evaluator share a family, so the "robustness utility" labels may be partially family-specific.
- **Ablation read.** Removing one perturbation type from training degrades the matched test slice but not the others; authors read this as "orthogonality of cognitive errors." Per-type training volume is modest (~6.7k queries with ~3.3k of each remaining type after lesion), so the diagonal degradation could partially reflect data volume rather than purely orthogonality.
- **Efficiency claim.** CoRM-RAG occupies the upper-left Pareto point in Figure 7 — +11.7% Biased-NQ accuracy over the Cross-Encoder at comparable latency (215 ms vs 210 ms), +1.6% over LLM-Rerank at 93× lower latency.
- **Hedging the paper does and does not preserve.** The paper uses strong verbs ("demonstrate," "significantly outperforms," "successfully decouples") and frames the contribution as a "paradigm shift for RAG: from information finding to causal intervention." The empirical apparatus is single-run point estimates on author-constructed adversarial sets; the rhetoric is larger than the apparatus.
- **Open questions flagged in the extraction.** (i) No seed counts or significance tests behind "significantly outperforms"; the +4.6 listwise-vs-pointwise gap is the central methodological claim. (ii) Teacher-LLM labels treated as ground truth without testing whether the Teacher is itself sycophantic on the perturbed queries. (iii) Biased-NQ is constructed by GPT-4o from NQ test items, which raises contamination risk specifically for the GPT-4o-generator transfer result. (iv) "Robustness" drifts between the formal `U(d,x)`, the Robustness Gap metric, and "evidential robustness" of documents.
