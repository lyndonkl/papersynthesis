# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Defends dense-retriever RAG against corpus-poisoning attacks by stress-testing each query-passage pair under perturbations and using the retriever's gradient response as an instability signal for reranking.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
ProGRank introduces a post-hoc, training-free retriever-side defense for dense-retriever RAG that preserves the original passage content and requires no retraining. It extracts probe-gradients from a small fixed parameter subset of the retriever under randomized perturbations and derives two instability signals (representational consistency, dispersion risk), combining them with a score gate in a reranking step. Across three datasets, three retriever backbones, multiple poisoning attacks, and both retrieval-stage and end-to-end settings, ProGRank shows stronger defence and a favorable robustness-utility trade-off, including under adaptive evasive attacks.

## 300 ft (specifics with hedging preserved, one sentence)
Training-free defense reranker that probes the retriever's gradient response under perturbation; competitive even under adaptive attacks, with a surrogate variant for unavailable retrievers.
