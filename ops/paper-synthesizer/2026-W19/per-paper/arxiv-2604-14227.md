# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Reveals a systematic bias in current rerankers — they prefer older, semantically rich documents over factually recent ones — and offers an instruction-optimization recipe that recovers up to +27% on temporally-evolving knowledge.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
FRESCO (Factual Recency and Evolving Semantic COnflict) is a benchmark for evaluating rerankers in temporally dynamic contexts, pairing recency-seeking queries with historical Wikipedia revisions. The empirical finding: every existing reranker tested exhibits a strong bias toward older documents even when they are factually obsolete. To mitigate, the authors search for Pareto-optimal instructions that balance evolving and non-evolving knowledge tasks, recovering up to 27% on Evolving Knowledge while maintaining competitive Non-Evolving performance.
