## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
HERA argues existing multi-agent RAG suffers from static agent behavior and fixed orchestration. The framework operates hierarchically: at the global level, query-specific agent topologies are optimized through reward-guided sampling and experience accumulation; at the local level, Role-Aware Prompt Evolution refines individual agent behaviors via credit assignment along operational and behavioral axes. Across six knowledge-intensive benchmarks HERA achieves +38.69% average over recent baselines while maintaining robustness and token efficiency, with topological analyses revealing emergent self-organization (sparse exploration yields compact, high-utility multi-agent networks).
