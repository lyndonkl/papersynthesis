# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Negative-result diagnostic: in cold-start movie recommendation, popularity-rank beats LLM-based cross-encoder rerankers by ~30x on HR@10, and the bottleneck is candidate-pool retrieval, not reranker capacity.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
This study runs controlled experiments on Serendipity-2018 with 500 users across multiple seeds and identifies three failure modes for cross-encoder rerankers in cold-start recommendation: low retrieval coverage (recall@200 = 0.109 vs 0.609 for baseline), severe exposure bias (rerankers recommend 3 unique items vs 497 for random), and minimal score discrimination between relevant and irrelevant items (Cohen's d = 0.13). Popularity-based ranking dominates LLM reranking (HR@10 0.268 vs 0.008, p<0.001) — the paper attributes the gap to retrieval-stage limits and offers practical mitigations (hybrid retrieval, candidate-pool sizing, score calibration).
