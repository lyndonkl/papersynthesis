# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Distills a corpus offline into a hierarchical skill directory with LLM-written summaries at each level, then lets an agent navigate the tree at serve time — outperforming dense retrieval, RAPTOR, and agentic-RAG baselines on enterprise QA.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
Corpus2Skill argues that RAG treats the LLM as a passive consumer of search results — it never sees how the corpus is organized or what it has not yet retrieved. The compilation pipeline iteratively clusters documents, generates LLM-written summaries at each level, and materializes a tree of navigable skill files; at serve time the agent gets a bird's-eye view, drills into branches via progressively finer summaries, and retrieves full documents by ID. On WixQA (enterprise customer-support RAG benchmark) Corpus2Skill outperforms dense retrieval / RAPTOR / agentic-RAG across all quality metrics; across nine RAGBench subsets it attains the highest macro-F1, and characterizes single-domain atomic-document corpora as the regime where corpus navigation is the right primitive.
