# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Trains an RL agent that adjusts its search depth per query by scoring intermediate self-generated answers, finding the minimal-sufficient depth and penalizing over-searching.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
AutoSearch attacks the redundant-search problem in agentic RAG: prior work limits search depth to reduce cost, but a fixed cap leads to underexploration of complex questions. AutoSearch first investigates how search depth affects accuracy and identifies a minimal-sufficient depth that defines a query-specific accuracy-efficiency trade-off (jointly determined by question complexity and the agent's capability). The RL framework rewards attainment of that depth and penalizes over-searching, with stabilizing rewards for behavior and answer quality, achieving a superior accuracy-efficiency trade-off across benchmarks.

## 300 ft (specifics with hedging preserved, one sentence)
