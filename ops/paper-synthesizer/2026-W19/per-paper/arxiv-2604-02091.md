# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Aligns reranker training to the LLM's downstream answer quality via reinforcement learning, replacing static human relevance labels with generation-utility feedback.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
RRPO (ReRanking Preference Optimization) argues that conventional rerankers optimize for relevance metrics that misalign with what the generator actually needs. It formulates reranking as sequential decision-making and uses LLM feedback as the reward, eliminating the need for human annotations; a reference-anchored deterministic baseline stabilizes training. Across knowledge-intensive benchmarks RRPO outperforms strong baselines including the listwise reranker RankZephyr, generalizes to GPT-4o readers, integrates with Query2Doc, and remains robust to noisy supervisors.

## 300 ft (specifics with hedging preserved, one sentence)
RL-trains rerankers using LLM-generation feedback as the reward (no human labels), with a reference-anchored deterministic baseline; outperforms RankZephyr on knowledge-intensive QA.
