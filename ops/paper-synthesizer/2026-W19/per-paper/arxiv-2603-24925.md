# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Adds graph-based reranking that captures non-semantic proximity between candidate documents while running over standard vector stores — no knowledge graph required.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
GraphER positions itself between (a) agentic iterative retrieval (which expands the search space but ignores corpus structure) and (b) full knowledge-graph systems (which capture richer proximity but break vector-store production deployment). It enriches data objects offline during indexing and performs graph-based reranking over candidates at query time; the design is retriever-agnostic with claimed negligible latency overhead. On multiple retrieval benchmarks, GraphER demonstrates effectiveness over standard baselines.
