# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Decomposes failure handling for agentic RAG into trajectory-level diagnosis + tool-conditioned local repair, reusing validated reasoning prefixes instead of rerunning the full pipeline.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
Doctor-RAG observes that as agentic-RAG reasoning trajectories grow longer, failures become common, and existing approaches either stop at diagnosis or rerun the entire pipeline (expensive). DR-RAG separates two stages: (i) trajectory-level failure diagnosis localizes errors via a coverage-gated taxonomy and identifies the earliest failure point; (ii) tool-conditioned local repair intervenes only at the diagnosed failure point, maximally reusing validated reasoning prefixes and retrieved evidence. Across three multi-hop QA benchmarks and multiple agentic-RAG baselines, DR-RAG substantially improves accuracy while significantly reducing reasoning token consumption versus rerun-based repair.
