## Headline
LegalMALR is a Chinese statute-retrieval pipeline that replaces the single-query dense-retrieval + lightweight-rerank pattern with a six-agent query-understanding loop (stabilised by reinforcement learning) plus a zero-shot LLM reranker, and reports +4-9 absolute points across Recall@10 / MRR@10 / nDCG@10 on two benchmarks against the strongest in-house RAG baseline.

## Summary
The authors argue that colloquial, implicit, multi-issue legal queries break conventional RAG pipelines because dense retrievers anchor on the query's literal surface and lightweight rerankers do not carry legal-reasoning capacity. LegalMALR closes the gap with two stacked components: a Multi-Agent Query Understanding System (MAS) that iteratively produces complementary reformulations (six specialised roles, planner-driven loop, max 4 rounds), and a zero-shot Qwen-Max reranker that scores the merged candidate pool with no chain-of-thought (deterministic JSON output only). The MAS policy is stabilised with a REINFORCE-style group-normalised optimisation the paper calls GRPO, trained via LoRA on Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 against a trajectory-level recall reward. Headline results: on the public STARD benchmark, Recall@10 = 0.8195 and MRR@10 = 0.7367 (+6.16 / +6.31 over the strongest in-house RAG baseline Qwen3-Original); on the authors' new CSAID benchmark (118 difficult queries, 7.16 gold statutes/query), Recall@10 = 0.6841 and MRR@10 = 0.9161, with +4.41 to +8.71 absolute points across metrics over the same baseline. The framework is presented as one that "jointly enhances recall, interpretive robustness, and final ranking accuracy" — though as the extraction notes, interpretive robustness is a design claim rather than a measured property.

## Specifics

- **Architecture, in three stacking rows (Fig. 2).** Row 1: baseline dense retrieval + lightweight cross-encoder rerank. Row 2: MAS + LLM Reranker. Row 3: MAS + GRPO + LLM Reranker. The two contributions are demonstrated as orthogonal — each ablation axis adds points on its own (Tables 7 and 8).
- **The six MAS agent roles (Table 2 / App. B Table 10).** Planner, Single-Element Rewrite, Supplementary-Element Rewrite, Multi-Element Decomposition, Supportive-Law Rewrite, Semantic-Abnormality Repair. The Planner inspects current candidate-set coverage and picks the next Rewrite Agent; the loop terminates after at most 4 reformulation rounds + 1 exit iteration.
- **GRPO formulation (Eqs. 1-7), with a caveat.** The optimisation is a REINFORCE-style estimator with K=8 trajectories per query, group-wise mean/std reward normalisation, and no value function. Reward = terminal MAS-stage recall + per-step −0.05 penalty + intermediate hit reward + −5 fallback penalty for malformed actions. Trained ~16h on 8x RTX 4090 with LoRA only, backbone frozen. The extraction flags that this formulation has no KL penalty to a reference policy and no PPO-style ratio clipping, which makes it a naming collision with the better-known DeepSeek-Math GRPO; the paper cites no GRPO source. The intermediate-hit-reward shaping coefficient `α` is described only as "a small shaping coefficient" with no numerical value, which limits reproducibility from the paper alone.
- **STARD test results (Table 5).** LegalMALR Recall@10 = 0.8195, MRR@10 = 0.7367. Strongest in-house RAG baseline (Qwen3-Original): 0.7579 / 0.6736 — a moderate ~6-point absolute margin. Non-RAG baselines from the original STARD publication look much weaker (Dense-STARD 0.6061 / 0.4724) but those numbers are reproduced rather than re-run, so the bigger gap is a "trust-the-prior-paper" comparison, not a head-to-head.
- **CSAID test results (Table 6).** LegalMALR best on every metric — Recall@10 = 0.6841, MRR@10 = 0.9161, nDCG@10 = 0.7126, HitRate@10 = 0.9915. Margin over Qwen3-SFT is +5.18 / +4.98 / +7.15 across the first three. CSAID is the authors' own benchmark (118 queries, three sources: 60 from a commercial retrieval log, 30 written by legal researchers, 28 from documented retrieval failures), so this is the standard author-released-benchmark caveat; the MAS policy is trained only on STARD's 1,234 training queries and CSAID is evaluated zero-shot, which partially addresses the concern.
- **The two ablations isolate each component (Tables 7 and 8).** MAS+GRPO lifts MAS-stage Recall@14 from 0.8012 (zero-shot MAS) to 0.8396 on STARD and from 0.6969 to 0.7478 on CSAID at a matched ~60-embedding-call budget. Swapping Qwen3-Reranker for the Qwen-Max LLM reranker on a fixed candidate set lifts Recall@10 from 0.7230 to 0.8101 on STARD-dense and from 0.5531 to 0.6329 on CSAID-dense. The LLM-R lift is larger when the candidate set is noisier (Qwen3-E 60c: +8.7) than when MAS has already filtered (MAS 14c: +4.3), suggesting LLM-R's edge comes partly from filtering irrelevant candidates that MAS has already largely removed.
- **The K-mismatch caveat in Fig. 4.** One-step MAS is reported at K=10, multi-step and MAS+GRPO at K=14. The paper rationalises this as "dynamic candidate pool sizes (approximately 14 on average)" but the extraction flags that this conflates two effects (more reformulations + more candidates retained), making the visual gap less clean than it looks.
- **The "no-CoT" reranking choice (Sec 3.4).** Qwen-Max emits only a JSON index list — no rationale. The paper reports that rationale-generation gave "modest" gains but cost latency, format stability, and fair-comparison clarity. The honest consequence: the "legal reasoning" claim rests on downstream retrieval metrics, not on inspected reasoning traces.
- **Why this is an agent-retrieval paper, not a classical IR paper.** The optimisation target is downstream applicability of statutes for an LLM-driven pipeline, not classical IR relevance — the rerank step explicitly invokes a commercial LLM (Qwen-Max) for legal-reasoning judgments at inference, and the MAS loop is itself an iterative multi-step retrieval policy with the Planner agent deciding whether to issue another retrieval round. This is the agentic-RAG + LLM-listwise-rerank pattern, applied to a domain (Chinese civil-law statute retrieval) where surface-form mismatch between query and corpus is the binding constraint.
- **What's not measured.** No human evaluation or legal-correctness audit of reranker output; the no-CoT choice deliberately makes Qwen-Max's reasoning uninspectable. The behavioural-category analysis in Table 4 (763 stable-correct, 186 occasionally-unstable, 78 never-fully-correct, 207 consistently-incorrect, summing to STARD's 1,234 training queries) is on training rollouts, not held-out data. MAS agent prompts are summarised in English in App. B, not disclosed verbatim — the actual Chinese prompts may be recoverable from the code release at github.com/lyxx3rd/LegalMALR but are not auditable from the paper alone.

## Why this matters in this run's context
Anchors the established "multi-agent query understanding pairs with LLM rerankers as the agentic-RAG default" sub-theme — the six-role MAS plus zero-shot Qwen-Max reranker is the run's clearest two-stage demonstration of the cohort's pattern, with ablations isolating MAS and LLM-reranker contributions as orthogonal.
