# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Reveals that the value of adaptive retrieval depends on model strength: it acts as a noise filter for weaker models and as a cost-aware efficiency lever for stronger reasoning models.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
AdaRankLLM proposes an adaptive listwise reranker built around a zero-shot prompt with passage-dropout, then introduces a two-stage progressive distillation paradigm to give smaller open-source LLMs the same capability. The empirical finding across three datasets and eight LLMs: adaptive retrieval consistently achieves optimal performance with significantly reduced context overhead, and — crucially — its functional role shifts with model strength, which the authors argue invites a re-evaluation of when adaptive RAG is even necessary.
