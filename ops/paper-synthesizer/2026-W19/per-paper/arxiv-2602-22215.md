## Headline
GYWI is a four-module retrieval pipeline that grounds an LLM's scientific-ideation prompts in a co-authorship knowledge graph, fusing dense RAG with GraphRAG and gating prompt updates by paired-t-test against a five-dimension score.

## Summary
The paper argues that LLM-generated research ideas fail on two specific axes — opaque inspiration provenance and weak controllable academic grounding — and proposes that an author-centred co-authorship knowledge graph, when used as the retrieval substrate behind a hybrid RAG + GraphRAG stack and a reinforcement-learning-styled prompt optimiser, materially improves the resulting ideas. Evidence comes from four parallel evaluations on a 1,000-paper arXiv-AI (2018-2023) benchmark: an IMCQ multiple-choice task where GYWI on DeepSeek-V3 reaches 93.94% accuracy versus an 83.84% DeepSeek-V3 base (≈10 pp absolute) and exceeds GPT-4o (87.88%), Gemini 2.5 Flash (86.87%), and Qwen3-8B (84.84%); a single-backbone module ablation; LLM-judge and blind-human five-dimension scoring; and a t-SNE Sentence-BERT semantic-space visualisation interpreted as showing concentrated-and-distinct idea distributions. The authors' headline claim that GYWI "significantly outperforms mainstream LLMs" is supported on a single backbone, single seed, single domain (arXiv-AI), and uses the same DeepSeek-V3 family as both backbone and LLM judge — a self-preference risk the paper does not control for.

## Specifics
- **System shape (four modules, sequential).** (A) *Author Knowledge Graph*: extract seed papers, build a co-author graph, run Louvain community detection, merge communities containing seeds into `A*`, collect all their papers as `P*`. (B) *Inspiration Source Sampling*: at hop depth `N` (running example uses N=1), draw `k_adj` adjacent-author papers (exploitation) and `k_rand` random papers from `P*` (exploration / the "eureka jump" component). Specific `k_adj`, `k_rand` values used in headline experiments are not disclosed. (C) *Hybrid Retrieval*: RAG for fine-grained factual fragments (depth), GraphRAG for cross-document community-level summaries (breadth). (D) *Adaptive Prompt Optimisation*: an RL-styled `Prompt_Optimizer` LLM iterates 5-10 rounds, scoring the mean of `[s_novelty, s_feasibility, s_clarity, s_relevance, s_significance]` (each 1-10) and accepting an update only when a paired-t-test on `N_eval = N_topic * R = 50` evaluations passes at α=0.05.

- **Module ablation (DeepSeek-V3 backbone, Fig. 5).** Base 83.84 → +RAG 88.89 (+5.05 pp) → +GraphRAG 89.90 (+1.01 pp) → +Prompt 93.94 (+4.04 pp). Authors read this as "knowledge graph + retrieval-generation is the dominant lift; prompt module merely helps the model exploit the retrieved context." Single seed implied; no error bars. The +1.01 pp from GraphRAG-after-RAG is small enough that the alternative reading — that the prompt optimiser is doing most of the post-retrieval work — is not ruled out.

- **Motivating measurement for using co-authorship as the graph backbone (Table I).** Average cosine similarity between co-authored paper titles 0.0604 vs 0.0134 for non-collaborative pairs (≈4.5x). Definition of "non-collaborative pairs" is not stated; both absolute values are low; no n, distribution, or significance test reported.

- **IMCQ task design.** For each of 1,000 evaluation papers, DeepSeek-V3 extracts the paper's "true motivation"; Sentence-BERT (all-mpnet-base-v2) picks 3 thematically-different distractors from AI Idea Bench; the generator picks the true motivation given title + abstract. 99 randomly selected target papers used in headline evaluation. Dataset statistics (Fig. 3) confirm IMCQ option lengths are length-balanced across A/B/C/D.

- **LLM-judge and human scoring (Figs. 6, 7).** LLM judge: DeepSeek-V3, GYWI 8.626 mean with highest scores in Novelty and Significance. Human: n=5 blind graduate students, GYWI 9.04 mean with Feasibility 9.5 and Significance 9.2. No inter-rater agreement statistic (κ, ICC) reported. LLM judge is the same family as the GYWI backbone — known self-preference risk, not controlled for.

- **Semantic-space diversity (Fig. 8, t-SNE on Sentence-BERT).** Twenty ideas per model per target. GYWI's points described as "more concentrated and distinct"; authors read this as semantic consistency plus diversity. Interpretation is qualitative — no cluster-tightness or between-cluster-distance metric is computed.

- **Stated limitations.** One-shot graph construction; no incremental / streaming graph update; cross-domain generalisation unproven (only arXiv-AI 2018-2023 tested); no comparison against other graph-RAG variants beyond the base-vs-RAG-vs-GraphRAG-vs-+prompt ablation; statistical significance is tested *only* inside the prompt-update gate, not for the headline cross-LLM IMCQ comparison or the ablation steps.

- **Goodhart concern.** The five dimensions (`novelty/feasibility/clarity/relevance/significance`) are used both as the reward inside the prompt-optimiser loop and as the scoring rubric for the LLM judge in Fig. 6 — the prompt is RL-trained against the metric on which it is later judged, by the same LLM family.

- **Agent angle (for this run's framing).** GYWI sits in the agentic-RAG / graph-RAG corner of the topic: the retrieval substrate is a structured scholarly graph rather than a flat vector store, and the system is built as a multi-module pipeline whose context-assembly step is itself governed by an LLM-driven optimiser. The Edge et al. 2024 GraphRAG technique is consumed wholesale; the contribution is the author-graph + exploit/explore sampling layer on top of it, plus the prompt-update loop.
