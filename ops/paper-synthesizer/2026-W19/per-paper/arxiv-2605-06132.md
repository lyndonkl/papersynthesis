# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Specializes a reranker to LLM-agent memory retrieval by distilling LLM-as-judge signals into a small (0.6B/4B) model that calibrates scores, handles temporal/causal queries, and exploits dialogue context.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
MemReranker argues generic similarity rerankers fail on agent-memory queries because they cannot reason — recalled passages are semantically close but lack the key fact. The fix is multi-stage LLM distillation: multi-teacher pairwise comparisons for calibrated soft labels, BCE pointwise distillation for well-distributed scores, and InfoNCE for hard-sample discrimination. The empirical claim is concrete: MemReranker-4B reaches 0.737 MAP on the memory-retrieval benchmark, matching Gemini-3-Flash on several metrics at 10-20% of the latency, with finance and healthcare benchmarks confirming generalization.
