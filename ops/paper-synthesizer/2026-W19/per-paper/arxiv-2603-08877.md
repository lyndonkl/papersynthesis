# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Provides a controlled measurement harness for budget-constrained agentic RAG and finds that hybrid lexical+dense retrieval with lightweight rerank gives the largest average accuracy gains under tool-call budgets.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
BCAS (Budget-Constrained Agentic Search) surfaces remaining budgets to the agent, gates tool use, and runs comparisons across six LLMs and three QA benchmarks. Three findings emerge: accuracy improves with additional searches up to a small cap (after which gains diminish), hybrid lexical+dense retrieval with lightweight rerank dominates the design grid, and larger completion budgets are most helpful on HotpotQA-style synthesis. The paper releases reproducible prompts and configurations.
