# Pass A — Within-paper layered summary

## 30,000 ft (paper's claim, one sentence)
Eliminates the embedder, vector index, and retrieval API entirely — the agent searches the raw corpus with grep, file reads, and shell commands — and reportedly outperforms strong sparse, dense, and reranking baselines on multiple BRIGHT, BEIR, BrowseComp-Plus and multi-hop QA datasets.

## 3,000 ft (argument + evidence, 2-3 sentences)
Direct Corpus Interaction (DCI) argues the conventional similarity-based retrieval interface is a bottleneck for agentic search: exact lexical constraints, sparse-clue conjunctions, local context checks, and multi-step hypothesis refinement are difficult to express via top-k retrieval, and evidence filtered out early cannot be recovered downstream. DCI's setup needs no offline indexing and adapts naturally to evolving local corpora. The empirical claim is striking: substantially outperforms strong sparse, dense, and reranking baselines on several BRIGHT and BEIR datasets, and attains strong accuracy on BrowseComp-Plus and multi-hop QA without any conventional semantic retriever.
