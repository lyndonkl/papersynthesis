## Run framing (what this run is about)

Classical systems thinking applied to **groups of individuals interacting, forming hierarchies, and producing complex interactive patterns**. The reader's goal is to bridge the *Thinking in Systems* tradition (Meadows / Forrester / Senge / Ashby / second-order cybernetics) into operational guidance for hierarchical multi-agent orchestrators they're building.

The reader's four working questions — these inform clustering and the per-cluster audience-reframe (handled by the style file):

1. What should they observe about such systems?
2. What helps improve them?
3. What are the failure modes and how to catch / categorize them early?
4. What automated feedback loops can they set up?

**Out-of-scope (drop with rationale):** papers whose primary subject is **LLM-based agents** (autonomous LLM agents, agentic workflows, LLM-orchestrated multi-agent systems). The reader is already current there and wants the underlying systems-theoretic vocabulary instead, not another LLM-agent eval paper.
