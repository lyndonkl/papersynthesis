# Retrieval and Re-ranking for LLM Agents — Highly Cited and Gaining Traction (Nov 2025 → May 2026)

## Headline

Across the last six months, the retrieval-and-reranking-for-LLM-agents cohort converges on one move: the optimization target shifts from query-document relevance to downstream LLM and agent utility. The established core (Nov 2025 → early Feb 2026) consolidates that shift inside multi-stage pipelines — structured retrieval substrates (LPG/Cypher, knowledge graphs, co-authorship graphs, encoding trees, tag paths) feeding agent-driven query understanding loops and LLM listwise rerankers — with Multi-Agent GraphRAG carrying the run's highest citation count (3) and GHAR, Higress-RAG, LegalMALR, and A-RAG anchoring the agentic-RAG and rerank-for-utility threads. The emerging edge (mid-Feb → May 2026) takes the same target one layer further: rerankers stop being scalar scorers and start emitting calibrated structured outputs (Prism-Reranker, Verbal-R3, CoRM-RAG, CAR, MemReranker), the retrieval primitive itself is contested (Don't Retrieve Navigate, Do We Still Need GraphRAG, Adaptive Query Routing, Hyper-RAG), and the first trajectory-level diagnostic benchmarks land (AgenticRAGTracer, BAGEL, the BioASQ controlled bake-off). What did not change across the window: there is still no shared agentic-RAG benchmark with seed-level variance, single-run point estimates dominate the headline tables, LLM judges share a family with the system under test in most papers, and the field continues to disagree on whether graph structure is necessary — single-shot multi-hop wants it (Do We Still Need GraphRAG), enterprise corpora often do not (Orion-RAG, Corpus2Skill).

## Established — papers with visible citations or adoption

### Graph-structured retrieval substrates become the agent's primitive

The established corner of this window pushes the agent's retrieval surface from a flat vector store to a structured graph or tree the agent can traverse — Cypher over LPG (Multi-Agent GraphRAG), meta-path partitions of biomedical KGs (GHAR), co-authorship graphs (GYWI), S²-entropy encoding trees (T-Retriever), and KG-RAG with causality-flavored calibration (Ca2KG). Multi-Agent GraphRAG carries the run's highest citation signal (3 S2 citations) and is the most-cited representative of this thread; GHAR adds the strongest cross-reference signal (cited by Hyper-RAG's lineage and the agentic-RAG taxonomy work). The shared move is not "use a graph" — it is "let an agent decide what to do with the graph, then collapse the iteration into one feedback step."

- **Multi-Agent GraphRAG: A Text-to-Cypher Framework for Labeled Property Graphs** — Gusarov et al., arxiv, 2025-11-11. A seven-agent bounded-refinement loop (capped at four iterations) translates natural-language questions into Cypher over LPG and improves accuracy by +6.79 to +10.23 pp over a same-model multi-attempt baseline across four LLM backbones on CypherBench; no per-agent ablation. *Traction:* 3 S2 citations — the most-cited paper in this run's kept set; posted 25 weeks ago. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.08274) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2511-08274.md)
- **Grounded by Experience: Generative Healthcare Prediction Augmented with Hierarchical Agentic Retrieval (GHAR)** — Zhao et al., arxiv, 2025-11-17. Two role-specialized agents share one Qwen2.5-3B backbone and are co-trained under multi-agent PPO over PrimeKG meta-paths; on three MIMIC-style datasets across three tasks, GHAR consistently outperforms 15 baselines on point estimates with the largest ablation drop from removing meta-path partitioning. *Traction:* 1 S2 citation; posted 24 weeks ago; cross-references the same graph-RAG lineage Multi-Agent GraphRAG and T-Retriever sit in. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.13293) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2511-13293.md)
- **Graph Your Way to Inspiration: Co-Author Graphs + RAG for Scientific Idea Generation (GYWI)** — Xie & Liang, arxiv, 2025-12-05. Fuses dense RAG with GraphRAG over a Louvain-clustered co-authorship graph and gates a prompt-optimizer's updates by paired-t-test on a five-dimension score; reaches 93.94% IMCQ accuracy on arXiv-AI (DeepSeek-V3 backbone, ≈10 pp over base) on a single backbone, single domain, single seed. *Traction:* 0 S2 citations but in the established tier on age (22 weeks ago) plus multi-lane match (dense + graph-RAG). → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22215) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2602-22215.md)
- **T-Retriever: Tree-based Hierarchical RAG for Textual Graphs** — Wei et al., arxiv, 2026-01-08. Replaces bottom-up community detection with top-down Shannon-Fano-style partitioning under a joint structural-plus-semantic entropy objective; reports +2.36 to +6.63% accuracy over the strongest hierarchical baseline on three textual-graph QA benchmarks, with the joint objective (0.7715) clearly beating semantic-only (0.6319) and structural-only (0.7154) on WebQSP. *Traction:* 0 S2 citations; posted 17 weeks ago; reads as the closest methodological successor to RAPTOR / ArchRAG inside the run's graph-RAG thread. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04945) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2601-04945.md)
- **When to Trust: Causality-Aware Calibration for KG-RAG (Ca2KG)** — Ren et al., arxiv, 2026-01-14. Reframes prompt variants as do-interventions over a KG-RAG pipeline and selects the answer that maximizes a Causal Calibration Index trading high mean support against low variance across interventions; wins 15 of 16 cells on Accuracy / ECE / Brier / selective AUC on MetaQA and WebQSP, with the strongest contribution attributed to the Path-Quality counterfactual prompt. *Traction:* 0 S2 citations; posted 16 weeks ago; one of two papers in the kept set targeting retrieval-conditioned confidence as the deliverable rather than rank order. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.09241) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2601-09241.md)

### Hybrid retrieval pipelines aim explicitly at downstream LLM utility

This sub-theme is the production-stack consolidation of the cohort's through-line: pipelines that interleave sparse, dense, and structural signals, fuse them by RRF, and reach for LLM utility at the end of the chain rather than IR relevance at the front. The strongest representative is the TREC TOT 2025 result, where round-robin fusion of BM25 + BGE-M3 + LLM-title-generation followed by a Gemini-2.5-flash listwise reranker wins, while a LambdaMART learned reranker collapses on the test set despite working on dev — a direct empirical signal that the LLM-utility target separates from the IR-relevance target. Higress-RAG and Orion-RAG sit in the same tier as engineering blueprints for the pattern, with Orion-RAG arguing the graph itself can be replaced by a per-chunk tag path.

- **Higress-RAG: Dual Hybrid Retrieval, Adaptive Routing, and CRAG** — Lin, arxiv, 2025-12-30. Four-layer enterprise stack exposed via MCP that combines Markdown-AST splitting, an adaptive Simple/Complex/External router, bge-m3 dense + BM25 sparse fused by RRF (k=60), a Milvus-native boost ranker, a bge-reranker-v2-m3 cross-encoder, and a CRAG corrective layer; reports ~+30 pp Hit Rate and ~+22 pp Factuality over an unspecified baseline on two proprietary in-domain test sets. *Traction:* 1 S2 citation; posted 18 weeks ago; placed in established on cross-lane match (Q4 dense + Q5 query-rewriting) and as the integration blueprint other production-RAG papers reference. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23374) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2602-23374.md)
- **Orion-RAG: Path-Aligned Hybrid Retrieval for Graphless Data** — Chen et al., arxiv, 2026-01-08. Argues retrieval over fragmented enterprise corpora does not need a knowledge graph — a per-chunk Master-Tag → Paragraph-Tag string embedded as a third index alongside dense and sparse, fused by RRF, suffices; reports +25.2% relative Precision@10 on FinanceBench over the strongest baseline and 8.6× lower latency than ReAct-RAG at 8-way concurrency on SeaCompany. *Traction:* 0 S2 citations; posted 17 weeks ago; established by age plus its explicit counter-argument to the graph-RAG cluster directly above. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04764) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2601-04764.md)
- **DS@GT at TREC TOT 2025: Fusion Retrieval and Learned Reranking** — Zhou et al., arxiv, 2026-01-21. The team's winning configuration on the TREC 2025 Tip-of-the-Tongue track interleaves BM25, BGE-M3 dense, and an LLM title-generation retriever via round-robin merge, then reranks the top-500 with a Gemini-2.5-flash listwise reranker (R@1000 = 0.6559, NDCG@1000 = 0.4106); a LambdaMART reranker that helps on dev collapses on test (RR 0.2838 → 0.0601), which the authors flag but do not fully diagnose. *Traction:* 0 S2 citations; posted 15 weeks ago; established as a competition-validated result that an LLM listwise reranker beats a learned reranker on the LLM-utility target. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15518) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2601-15518.md)

### Multi-agent query understanding pairs with LLM rerankers as the agentic-RAG default

By early February the established line is no longer "retrieve then rerank" — it is "iterate query understanding inside an agent loop, then rerank with an LLM that scores for downstream utility." LegalMALR demonstrates the pattern in Chinese statute retrieval with a six-role MAS plus a zero-shot Qwen-Max reranker; A-RAG exposes the agentic surface as three hierarchical tools (keyword_search, semantic_search, chunk_read) and reports that the agent itself orchestrating the tools beats graph-RAG and workflow-RAG baselines on multi-hop QA at comparable or fewer retrieved tokens. The two papers triangulate the established shape of agentic-RAG: iteration is done by the agent, ranking is done by an LLM scoring for utility.

- **LegalMALR: Multi-Agent Query Understanding and LLM-Based Reranking for Chinese Statute Retrieval** — Li et al., arxiv, 2026-01-25. A six-role MAS (Planner + five specialised Rewrite agents, max 4 rounds) stabilised by a REINFORCE-style group-normalised optimisation plus a zero-shot Qwen-Max LLM reranker; reports +4-9 absolute points across Recall@10 / MRR@10 / nDCG@10 over the strongest in-house RAG baseline on STARD and on a new CSAID benchmark, with the two contributions demonstrated as orthogonal in ablation. *Traction:* S2 lookup unavailable; posted 15 weeks ago; composite relevance score 17 with multi-lane match (Q2 LLM-reranker, Q4 dense, Q5 query-rewriting). → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17692) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2601-17692.md)
- **A-RAG: Scaling Agentic RAG via Hierarchical Retrieval Interfaces** — Du et al., arxiv, 2026-02-03. Exposes three hierarchical tools (keyword_search, semantic_search, chunk_read) over a two-stage lightweight index inside a ReAct loop with a context tracker that suppresses re-reads; outperforms graph-RAG and workflow-RAG baselines on four multi-hop QA datasets at comparable or fewer retrieved tokens, with the failure-mode breakdown shifting from "couldn't find documents" to "found documents, reasoned wrong." *Traction:* S2 lookup unavailable; posted 13 weeks ago; established on cross-lane match (Q1 agentic-RAG, Q6 graph-RAG) and on direct reference in later kept papers' positioning. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03442) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2602-03442.md)

## Emerging — recent work where traction is pre-citation

### Rerankers shift from scalar scoring to structured outputs for the LLM consumer

This is the most coherent emerging sub-theme and the direct next-generation of the established "LLM rerankers for downstream utility" thread. Five papers within seven weeks argue the reranker's interface itself is wrong: a scalar score wastes context tokens, gives the generator no actionable signal, and optimizes for the wrong target. Prism-Reranker emits a calibrated score plus an XML contribution + evidence rewrite in one forward pass; Verbal-R3 emits natural-language relevance annotations the generator is GRPO-aligned to consume; CoRM-RAG retrains the reranker to score by whether documents steer the LLM to the correct answer under adversarial query rewrites; CAR rescores documents by how much each shifts the generator's answer-consistency without any training; MemReranker carries the same pattern into agent memory retrieval. Connection to the established tier: this is the rerank-for-utility thread maturing — the established papers used an off-the-shelf LLM as a listwise reranker; the emerging papers redesign the reranker itself.

- **Prism-Reranker: Beyond Relevance Scoring — Jointly Producing Contributions and Evidence for Agentic Retrieval** — Zhang, arxiv, 2026-04-26. Four-size Qwen3.5 family (0.8B/2B/4B/9B) that reads a calibrated score from logits at the prompt boundary and autoregressively continues into `<contribution>` + `<evidence>` only on positives, so tail negatives cost one token; the same recipe applied as self-distillation to Qwen3-Reranker-4B lifts BEIR-QA NDCG@10 from 57.33 to 58.87 (+1.54), with the paper itself flagging the gain most plausibly comes from the SFT branch's ensemble-label supervision. *Traction:* Posted 2 weeks ago, pre-citation; single-author preprint but with model release at four sizes plus cached teacher scores — kept on the strength of the no-cost-on-negatives interface design + reproducible release. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23734) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2604-23734.md)
- **Beyond Semantic Relevance: Counterfactual Risk Minimization for Robust RAG (CoRM-RAG)** — Liu et al., arxiv, 2026-05-02. Retrains a DeBERTa-v3-large cross-encoder to score documents by whether they steer the LLM to the correct answer under simulated bias rewrites (False Premise / Confirmation Bias / Topical Distraction), distilled from a Teacher LLM's reliability labels on ~50k listwise groups; reports 52.6% on Biased-NQ vs 40.9% for a stock MS-MARCO Cross-Encoder at 215 ms latency vs ~20 s for an LLM reranker. *Traction:* Posted 1 week ago, pre-citation; kept on the strength of a named adversarial setup + retrieval-target reframing that lines up directly with Prism-Reranker and Verbal-R3 on the same week. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01302) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2605-01302.md)
- **Verbal-R3: Verbal Reranker as the Missing Bridge between Retrieval and Reasoning** — Park et al., arxiv, 2026-05-02. Inserts a 1.5B/3B Verbal Reranker distilled from GPT-OSS-120B between retrieval and the generator; the reranker emits both a 1-5 score and a natural-language Verbal Annotation, and the generator is GRPO-aligned to consume it — reports +17.1% EM / +18.0% F1 over Search-R1 3B on average across seven QA benchmarks, with the reranker scoring below standard baselines on BEIR nDCG@10 yet outperforming them end-to-end. *Traction:* Posted 1 week ago, pre-citation; the BEIR-vs-downstream gap is itself the headline signal that the rerank target has shifted off classical IR relevance. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01399) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2605-01399.md)
- **CAR: Query-Guided Confidence-Aware Reranking for RAG** — Song et al., arxiv, 2026-05-06. Training-free reranker that rescores documents by how much each shifts the generator's answer-consistency (max-cluster proportion across k bidirectional-entailment-clustered samples), gated by a Query Threshold so already-confident queries keep their baseline order; reports non-negative NDCG@5 lifts across all (reranker, dataset) cells with very large gains on weak LLM-prompted rerankers and near-zero lifts on strong supervised ones. *Traction:* Posted 4 days ago, pre-citation; kept on the strength of the training-free utility signal + the explicit conservatism gate that protects strong baselines. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04495) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2605-04495.md)
- **MemReranker: Reasoning-Aware Reranking for Agent Memory Retrieval** — Li et al., arxiv, 2026-05-07. 0.6B/4B Qwen3-Reranker-derived family distilled via a four-stage pipeline (Rank-DistiLLM capability preservation → pairwise LLM teacher labels via Bradley-Terry → BCE pointwise distillation → InfoNCE listwise contrastive); MemReranker-0.6B matches GPT-4o-mini's LOCOMO MAP (0.7150 vs 0.7151) at roughly one-sixth the latency, with multi-hop reasoning the documented weakness. *Traction:* Posted 3 days ago, pre-citation; kept as the only paper in the kept set targeting agent memory retrieval specifically (Q3 lane). → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06132) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2605-06132.md)

### The retrieval primitive itself is being contested — graphs, navigation, routing, hypergraphs

Where the established tier mostly assumed the standard "embed → retrieve → rerank" pipeline and varied the structure of the index, the emerging tier asks whether the primitive is right. Four papers each argue a different alternative: agentic interaction supplies enough implicit structure to narrow but not close the dense-vs-graph gap (Do We Still Need GraphRAG); for atomic single-domain enterprise corpora, navigation over a compiled skill forest beats retrieval (Corpus2Skill); for documents whose complexity varies, an LLM router should dispatch by query tier to vector / tree / fused backends (Adaptive Query Routing); for domains with many-way correlations, the pairwise graph itself should become a hypergraph (Hyper-RAG). These are not the same answer — they form a tension. Connection to the established tier: this is the structured-substrate thread above either being defended with a benchmark (Do We Still Need GraphRAG, on multi-hop) or rejected for specific corpus shapes (Corpus2Skill, Adaptive Query Routing).

- **Do We Still Need GraphRAG? Benchmarking RAG and GraphRAG for Agentic Search Systems** — Fan et al., arxiv, 2026-04-01. Releases RAGSearch, a benchmark holding the agentic control layer fixed (ReAct) while swapping the retrieval backend across dense RAG and five GraphRAG variants under single-shot, training-free agentic, and RL-trained regimes; reports the multi-hop dense-vs-GraphRAG Contain-EM gap shrinks from +27.23 (single-shot) to +26.59 (under GraphSearch), which the authors describe as "partially mitigated" — explicit graph structure remains advantageous for multi-hop, with HippoRAG2 the consistent best backend. *Traction:* Posted 5 weeks ago, pre-citation; kept as the head-to-head benchmark the established graph-RAG cluster has been missing. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09666) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2604-09666.md)
- **Adaptive Query Routing: Tier-Based Framework for Hybrid Retrieval Across Financial, Legal, and Medical Documents** — Hashmi, arxiv, 2026-04-14. Formalises a four-tier query taxonomy (T1 single-fact → T4 multi-document synthesis) and benchmarks Vector RAG, Tree Reasoning, and an LLM-routed Adaptive Hybrid Retrieval across financial, legal, and medical documents; argues query complexity (not domain or paradigm) determines which retrieval strategy wins, with Tree Reasoning leading on aggregate quality (0.900 controlled, 0.938 FinanceBench) and AHR's value framed as catastrophic-failure avoidance plus latency Pareto-improvement rather than aggregate dominance. *Traction:* Posted 3 weeks ago, pre-citation; kept on the explicit "no paradigm dominates every tier" empirical claim that pushes back on the established tier's monolithic pipelines. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14222) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2604-14222.md)
- **Don't Retrieve, Navigate: Distilling Enterprise Knowledge into Navigable Agent Skills (Corpus2Skill)** — Sun et al., arxiv, 2026-04-16. Compiles a single-domain corpus offline into a filesystem-shaped hierarchy of LLM-written skill summaries (SKILL.md / INDEX.md / documents.json) and an agent top-down navigates it via two tools at serve time; on WixQA (n=200) leads F1 0.468 / Factuality 0.739 / Context Recall 0.673 over dense, hybrid, agentic-RAG, RAPTOR, and BM25, with the win explicitly scoped to atomic 0.5-3k-character documents and honest losses on TatQA tables and CUAD contracts. *Traction:* Posted 3 weeks ago, pre-citation; kept on the strength of the regime-scoped argument plus the explicit failure-region disclosure (cost parity is caching-conditional, 38 of 62 failures are top-level routing errors). → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14572) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2604-14572.md)
- **Hyper-RAG: Combating LLM Hallucinations Using Hypergraph-Driven RAG** — Feng et al., PubMed (Nature Communications), 2026-04-27. Replaces the pairwise knowledge graph in GraphRAG/LightRAG with a hypergraph where any hyperedge can connect ≥3 entities, so multi-entity correlations are encoded directly instead of lossy-projected; reports +12.3% over direct LLM use on NeurologyCorp (six LLMs, Scoring-Based), +35.5% over LightRAG across nine domains (Selection-Based), a 76% drop in the error-propagation coefficient α, and domain-expert correctness +7.1% with the gap widening on harder questions. *Traction:* Posted 1 week ago, pre-citation; kept as the highest-venue paper in this run (Nature Communications) and as the representational-change counterpart to the graph-RAG established thread. → [link](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42045193/) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/pubmed-42045193.md)

### Diagnostic and controlled-evaluation work exposes where agentic-RAG actually stands

The third emerging sub-theme is the field's belated calibration work: trajectory-level benchmarks for agentic retrieval reasoning (AgenticRAGTracer), Bayesian-active-learning frameworks that probe how far beyond top-K relevant passages live (BAGEL), and controlled bake-offs that hold every variable fixed except the retrieval strategy (the BioASQ study). The shared finding is that the field has been measuring the wrong thing: AgenticRAGTracer reports GPT-5 drops from 76.2 EM on 2-hop Comparison to 22.6 EM on 4-hop Inference and identifies decomposition (not retrieval) as the dominant failure mode; BAGEL shows recall at deeper cutoffs lifts substantially when LLM calls are spent exploring instead of reranking the dense retriever's top-K; the BioASQ study reports that Multi-Query Expansion *hurts* contextual precision (0.671) versus Dense baseline. This sub-theme has no direct established-tier analog — it is what the established tier did not produce.

- **AgenticRAGTracer: A Hop-Aware Benchmark for Diagnosing Multi-Step Retrieval Reasoning in Agentic RAG** — You et al., arxiv, 2026-02-22. 1,305-item benchmark built through a six-step automated pipeline with three-stage logical-fidelity verification (retrieval necessity, dependency irreducibility, grounded solvability) and three-annotator review at Fleiss' κ = 0.65; evaluates 13 LLMs under a unified Qwen-Agent / ReAct setup and reports GPT-5 at 22.6 EM on the 4-hop Inference split, with Steps-I drifting longer than Steps-C in almost every cell — read as quantitative evidence that failure is predominantly characterized by distorted reasoning chains rooted in brittle initial decomposition. *Traction:* Posted 11 weeks ago, pre-citation; kept as the first agentic-RAG benchmark with automated step-by-step trajectory validation (primacy claim qualified). → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.19127) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2602-19127.md)
- **BAGEL: Bayesian Active Learning with Gaussian Processes Guided by LLM Relevance Scoring for Dense Passage Retrieval** — Kim et al., arxiv, 2026-04-20. Fits a query-specific GP on dense passage embeddings using LLM relevance scores as observations, then uses UCB acquisition to spend a fixed budget of 50 LLM calls per query on exploration beyond the dense retriever's top-K; beats BM25, dense, cross-encoder, and pointwise/listwise LLM rerankers on NDCG@10/50 and Recall@10/50 across four BEIR-like benchmarks, with the deeper-cutoff (@50) cells carrying the strongest statistical evidence (Wilcoxon p<0.05 + bootstrap CIs above zero). *Traction:* Posted 2 weeks ago, pre-citation; kept on the strength of the "LLM-call budget as the binding constraint" framing that aligns directly with the rerank-for-utility emerging thread. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17906) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2604-17906.md)
- **Benchmarking Retrieval Strategies for Biomedical RAG: A Controlled Empirical Study** — Bal & Puhan, arxiv, 2026-05-04. Holds generator (GPT-4o-mini), embeddings, vector store, chunking, and top-k fixed and varies only the retrieval strategy across Dense, Hybrid BM25+Dense, Cross-Encoder Reranking, Multi-Query Expansion, and MMR on rag-mini-bioasq (250 QA, 95% CIs); Cross-Encoder leads composite (0.827) by only 0.005 over Dense and Multi-Query precision drops to 0.671 with a CI not overlapping any other strategy — the authors say this "suggests naive query diversification introduces retrieval noise." *Traction:* Posted just under 1 week ago, pre-citation; kept as the only paper in the kept set with seed-controlled CIs on every retrieval primitive an agent would compose. → [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02520) · [summary](../2026-W19/per-paper/arxiv-2605-02520.md)
