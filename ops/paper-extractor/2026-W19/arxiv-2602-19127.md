## Pass 2 — Content grasp

### Main argument and supporting evidence

The paper argues that existing multi-hop RAG benchmarks (HotpotQA, 2WikiMultihopQA, MuSiQue, BamboogleQA) cannot diagnose *where* agentic-RAG systems fail because they evaluate only the final answer, not the intermediate reasoning trajectory; and that prior LLM-generated alternatives (MoreHopQA, MultiHop-RAG, MINTQA) lack the fine-grained quality control to be reliable evaluation substrates. To remedy this, the authors construct AgenticRAGTracer via a six-step automated pipeline (initial Wikipedia filtering with explicit anti-overlap against existing benchmarks → atomic QA generation → atomic QA filtering by difficulty buckets → multi-hop QA generation via iterative merging into Sequential/Inference and Parallel/Comparison topologies → multi-hop QA filtering with three-stage verification (structural integrity, semantic logic, multi-hop necessity & solvability) → SPARQL disambiguation plus full-scale human review by three annotators with Fleiss' κ = 0.65), yielding 1,305 examples across 11 domains and four hop depths (2-, 3-, 4-hop) for both topologies. Evaluating 13 LLMs under a unified Qwen-Agent / ReAct setup with EM, F1, and LLM-as-Judge metrics (Table 1), the paper shows that performance degrades sharply with hop count — GPT-5 drops from 76.2 EM on 2-hop Comparison to 22.6 EM on 4-hop Inference, and the GPT-4o anomaly (15.6 EM on 4-hop Comparison vs o4-mini's 35.9) is explained by retrieval-volume analysis (Figure 3) showing GPT-4o's conservative top-k strategy starves the chain of evidence. The cross-hop analysis in Table 2 (Steps-C vs Steps-I) is the load-bearing diagnostic claim: correct trajectories have step counts close to the target hop number, while incorrect trajectories show both premature collapse and over-extension — supporting the conclusion that the dominant failure mode is *distorted reasoning chains driven by a brittle initial decomposition*, not isolated tool-use errors (Figure 4 error case).

### The Big Question

How do we evaluate, diagnose, and ultimately improve the *internal reasoning trajectory* of agentic RAG systems — not just whether they output the right final answer — given that the field has accelerated past the static retrieve-then-read paradigm into autonomous multi-step planning, tool use, and reflection, while evaluation infrastructure has stayed anchored on outcome-only multi-hop QA benchmarks built for traditional RAG?

### Specific design claims being defended

This is a benchmark/dataset paper, so the operative claims are design claims rather than scientific hypotheses:

1. *Authenticity claim* — that questions labeled n-hop in prior benchmarks frequently aren't truly n-hop (answerable from parametric knowledge or fewer hops), and that enforcing retrieval-necessity + dependency-irreducibility + grounded-solvability yields questions of higher logical fidelity (Appendix B presents the case studies; §3.3 defines the three criteria).
2. *Faithfulness-to-RAG-settings claim* — that releasing the corpus and retrieval index (the FlashRAG / ModelScope collection) is necessary for reproducible agentic-RAG evaluation, because retrieval performance is highly sensitive to index construction.
3. *Topology claim* — that splitting questions into Sequential/Inference (stepwise logical chain) and Parallel/Comparison (independent gathering then synthesis) topologies, each at 2/3/4-hop depths, exposes capabilities masked by single-axis multi-hop benchmarks.
4. *Diagnostic-utility claim* — that step-count diagnostics (MaxD, Steps-C, Steps-I) on the benchmark detect distorted reasoning chains (premature collapse and over-extension) that outcome-only benchmarks cannot detect.
5. *Quality claim* — that the automated pipeline plus human review (Fleiss' κ = 0.65) produces a benchmark with no overlap with HotpotQA/2Wiki/MuSiQue/BamboogleQA and substantial inter-annotator agreement.

### Unfamiliar terms / concepts requiring gloss for a non-specialist reader

- **Agentic RAG** — retrieval-augmented generation embedded in an autonomous-agent loop (plan → tool-call → reflect → iterate), as opposed to a single retrieve-then-read pass.
- **Hop / multi-hop** — the number of distinct retrieval/inference steps required to compose the final answer; "2-hop" means two chained retrievals must be combined.
- **Inference (Sequential) vs Comparison (Parallel) topology** — Inference questions require stepwise chaining where hop k's answer is needed to formulate hop k+1's query; Comparison questions require gathering facts about multiple entities independently and then comparing along a shared dimension.
- **Atomic QA pair** — a single-fact question/answer pair derived from one passage, used as a building block for synthesizing multi-hop questions.
- **Exact Match (EM) / F1 / LLM-as-Judge** — standard QA metrics: EM is string-equality with the gold answer, F1 is token overlap, LLM-as-Judge has an LLM (GPT-4o-mini at temperature 0 here) rate correctness.
- **ReAct paradigm** — a prompting pattern (Yao et al. 2023) that interleaves Reasoning traces with Acting (tool calls) within a single agent loop.
- **MaxD / Steps-C / Steps-I** — paper-defined diagnostics: MaxD = the highest hop depth a model still answers correctly; Steps-C = avg step count on correctly answered samples; Steps-I = avg step count on incorrectly answered samples.
- **Top-k retrieval** — the number of documents pulled per retrieval call; the paper uses Figure 3 to show top-k strategy correlates with end-task performance.
- **Fleiss' κ** — multi-rater inter-annotator agreement coefficient; κ = 0.65 indicates substantial agreement.
- **SPARQL disambiguation** — using Wikidata SPARQL queries to resolve cases where multiple entities share a name, removing ambiguous benchmark items.
- **Retrieval necessity / Dependency irreducibility / Grounded solvability** — paper's three-criteria filter ensuring a question (i) is not parametric-only, (ii) breaks if any supporting doc is removed, (iii) is solvable given the full evidence set.
- **FlashRAG dataset** — the open Wikipedia corpus and index released by an earlier RAG benchmarking effort; AgenticRAGTracer uses this same collection to ensure index-comparable evaluation.

### Confusions / unresolved points

- **Single-run evaluation, no seeds disclosed.** Table 1's EM/F1 numbers carry no error bars and the paper does not discuss multi-seed runs or temperature settings for the *evaluated* models (only that the LLM-judge runs at temperature 0). For numbers like GPT-5's 22.6 EM on n≈64 4-hop Inference items, run-to-run variance could be material.
- **The 4-hop bucket is small (~128 items total across both topologies; ~64 Inference).** The headline 22.6% figure rests on ~64 items, which deserves a confidence-interval discussion the paper omits.
- **Authenticity-claim asymmetry.** §2.2 + Appendix B critique MoreHopQA / MultiHop-RAG / MINTQA for "without ensuring fine-grained quality control," but the paper presents only positive case studies on its own pipeline and negative case studies on prior work — no symmetric quantitative comparison (e.g., what fraction of HotpotQA items fail the retrieval-necessity criterion under their own filter?).
- **GPT-4o anomaly is explained correlationally.** Figure 3 shows GPT-4o uses low top-k *and* performs poorly; the paper concludes the former causes the latter. No controlled experiment varies top-k while holding other factors fixed.
- **DeepSeek-R1 Steps-C/Steps-I inversion (4-hop Inference: 4.17/2.76) is unremarked.** This counter-example to the "incorrect = over-extended" narrative isn't reconciled with the global claim that distortion is both premature-collapse *and* over-extension.
- **"Distorted reasoning chains" causal language is strong.** The paper sometimes hedges ("failures are predominantly characterized by," "highlights an inability") and sometimes asserts ("not isolated errors in tool usage but rather cascading failures fundamentally rooted in a breakdown of initial task decomposition," §4.4). The single Figure 4 error case is an existence proof, not a frequency estimate; the global claim would benefit from a coded sample over many failure traces.
- **"First Agentic RAG benchmark automatically constructed with step-by-step validation" primacy claim** (intro + conclusion) is qualified to a specific definitional combination — earlier LLM-generated multi-hop benchmarks (MoreHopQA, MultiHop-RAG, MINTQA) do exist; the primacy hinges on what counts as "step-by-step validation." This is defensible but worth flagging as an asymmetric claim.
- **Appendix D referenced but not visible.** §4.4 says "additional details can be found in Appendix D" but the rendered PDF appears to jump from Appendix C (Prompts, Figures 5-10) to either an omitted Appendix D or material trimmed for arXiv release.
- **No release verification.** Code/data link given as `https://github.com/YqjMartin/AgenticRAGTracer`; not verified in this pass.
