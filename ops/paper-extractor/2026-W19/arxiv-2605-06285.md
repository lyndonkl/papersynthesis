## Pass 1 — Inspectional reading

### Five Cs

1. **Category** — System / architecture description (empirical efficiency claim). The paper introduces a named framework ("LatentRAG") and reports a quantitative inference-latency reduction against an "explicit agentic RAG" baseline, so the dominant frame is a system/method paper rather than a theoretical analysis or survey.

2. **Context** — Sits within the agentic RAG line of work, where LLM agents interleave retrieval calls with explicit reasoning tokens, and within the broader thread of "latent reasoning" / continuous-thought approaches (e.g., Coconut-style latent CoT) that try to move chain-of-thought off the discrete token stream. The abstract positions the contribution against "explicit agentic RAG" as the comparison point but does not name specific prior systems.

3. **Correctness (first-glance)** — No first-glance concerns from the abstract alone. The ~90% latency reduction is plausible mechanistically (skipping decoded reasoning/retrieval tokens removes many forward passes), and the claim is bounded to latency + "comparable" task performance rather than a strict accuracy win. The abstract is thin on the comparison protocol (which benchmarks, which baselines, what "comparable" means quantitatively), so a Pass 2 read is needed to verify the claim is not over-stretched.

4. **Contributions** — Claims (a) a framework that shifts both reasoning *and* retrieval into continuous latent space, with latent tokens "produced directly from hidden states," and (b) an empirical result of approximately 90% inference-latency reduction at "comparable" performance to explicit agentic RAG. Hedging is mild — "achieves performance comparable to" (not "matches" or "outperforms"), "reducing latency by approximately 90%" (approximate, single number). Preserve both qualifiers downstream.

5. **Clarity** — `dense-but-clear`. Title matches abstract; the abstract is unusually compressed (two sentences) and elides the mechanism for how retrieval-in-latent-space resolves the discrete vector-DB lookup that agentic RAG normally performs. Specific terms ("latent tokens," "hidden states") are standard in the latent-reasoning literature but the retrieval-in-latent-space claim is the load-bearing novelty and is not unpacked at the abstract level.

### One-line summary

LatentRAG moves both the reasoning and retrieval steps of an agentic RAG loop into continuous latent space — emitting latent tokens from hidden states rather than decoded text — and reports comparable task performance at roughly 90% lower inference latency.
