### Pass 2 question set

**1. Main argument and supporting evidence (3-5 sentences).**
The paper argues that LLM-generated research ideas suffer from two specific failures — opaque inspiration provenance and weak controllable academic grounding — and that constructing an author-centred co-authorship knowledge graph as the retrieval substrate, then fusing fine-grained RAG with structural GraphRAG, then governing prompt assembly by a reinforcement-learning-styled iterative optimiser, materially improves the resulting ideas. The supporting evidence is fourfold: (i) an IMCQ multiple-choice task in which GYWI on DeepSeek-V3 reaches 93.94% accuracy versus an 83.84% DeepSeek-V3 base (≈10 pp absolute), and exceeds GPT-4o (87.88%), Gemini 2.5 Flash (86.87%), and Qwen3-8B (84.84%); (ii) a single-backbone ablation showing +5.05 pp from adding RAG, +1.01 pp from adding GraphRAG, +4.04 pp from adding the prompt-optimisation module (Fig. 5), which the authors read as "knowledge graph + retrieval-generation is the dominant lift; prompt module merely helps the model exploit the retrieved context"; (iii) LLM-judge and blind human scoring on the five dimensions (LLM judge: GYWI 8.626 mean, highest in Novelty and Significance; human: GYWI 9.04 mean, with Feasibility 9.5 and Significance 9.2 well above all four baselines); (iv) a t-SNE Sentence-BERT visualisation interpreted as showing GYWI's idea distribution being more concentrated and distinct, claimed to reflect higher consistency-plus-diversity. The pairing of automatic, LLM-judged, human-judged, and visualisation evaluations addresses (but does not fully eliminate) the well-known LLM-judge-favours-its-own-family concern, since the LLM judge (DeepSeek-V3) is also the GYWI backbone in the ablation.

**2. The Big Question.**
Can LLM-based scientific ideation be made *traceable and academically grounded* — i.e., can the system's outputs be tied back to specific papers, specific co-author communities, and specific exploit/explore sampling decisions, rather than emerging from opaque pretraining priors — while still preserving the "eureka jump" character of creative inspiration? More broadly, the paper sits inside the active debate about whether *structural* (graph-level) retrieval over scholarly metadata adds value beyond *semantic* (chunk-level) dense retrieval for creative scientific tasks.

**3. Specific hypotheses / design claims tested.**
This is a systems paper without explicit hypothesis statements; the defended design claims are:
- H1: Replacing semantic-only retrieval with retrieval over an author-centred co-authorship graph yields more academically credible and traceable ideas. *Operationalised as*: IMCQ accuracy and five-dim mean score under GYWI exceed those of LLM-only and other LLM-based baselines.
- H2: Co-authors are a meaningfully tighter semantic cluster than random author pairs, justifying the use of co-authorship as the graph backbone. *Operationalised as*: Table I — avg cosine similarity between co-authored paper titles 0.0604 vs 0.0134 for non-collaborative pairs (≈4.5x).
- H3: A *hybrid* depth (RAG) + breadth (GraphRAG) retrieval beats either alone for ideation quality. *Operationalised as*: ablation in Fig. 5 (base 83.84 → +RAG 88.89 → +GraphRAG 89.90 → +prompt 93.94).
- H4: Both *exploitation* (adjacent N-hop sampling) and *exploration* (random sampling) inspiration sources are required to balance factual grounding against eureka-style novelty. *Operationalised as*: not directly ablated — there is no `k_adj=0` vs `k_rand=0` comparison; the claim is asserted by construction and supported only by the overall accuracy improvement.
- H5: An RL-styled prompt optimiser with a paired-t-test acceptance gate improves utilisation of the hybrid context beyond a single fixed prompt template. *Operationalised as*: +4.04 pp IMCQ gain in the ablation; prompt comparison in Appendix E shows the optimised prompt adds GraphRAG-summary plus RAG-chunks layering, synergy/gap-filling requirements, and provenance-aware fields.
- H6: GYWI produces ideas with higher semantic *diversity-with-consistency* than baselines. *Operationalised as*: qualitative read of the t-SNE figure (Fig. 8) — explicitly interpretive, no clustering-metric number provided.

**4. Unfamiliar terms / concepts requiring gloss.**
- *GraphRAG* — Microsoft's variant of retrieval-augmented generation in which the retrieval index is a knowledge graph (entities as nodes, relations as edges) rather than a flat vector store, enabling community-level summarisation across documents (Edge et al. 2024; Han et al. 2025 in refs [11], [12]).
- *Louvain community detection* — a modularity-optimisation algorithm for partitioning a graph into densely-connected communities (Blondel et al. 2008, ref [18]); used here to identify the "core academic community" around each seed author.
- *Author-centred knowledge graph (A\* ∪ P\*, E)* — the bipartite graph constructed here: `A*` is the author set obtained by merging Louvain communities containing seed authors `A_seed`; `P*` is all papers authored by anyone in `A*`; `E` represents author→paper authorship edges.
- *Inspiration Source Sampling — adjacent (exploitation) vs random (exploration)* — at depth `N`, sample `k_adj` papers from the author neighbourhood of a target paper (factually-related material) and `k_rand` papers from elsewhere in `P*` (the "eureka jump" component).
- *IMCQ — Idea Multiple-Choice Question evaluation* — for each of 1,000 evaluation papers, DeepSeek-V3 extracts the *true motivation* of the paper; Sentence-BERT picks 3 thematically-different distractors from AI Idea Bench; the generator's task is to pick the true motivation given the title + abstract.
- *AI Idea Bench (2025)* — prior benchmark by Qiu et al., ref [7], which GYWI both inherits the evaluation philosophy from and draws distractors from.
- *Sentence-BERT (all-mpnet-base-v2)* — sentence-embedding model used both for selecting IMCQ distractors and for the t-SNE semantic-space diversity analysis.
- *Five-dimension scoring vector* — `S(P) = [s_novelty, s_feasibility, s_clarity, s_relevance, s_significance]` each on 1-10, optimised as the mean `S̄`.
- *Paper hop ordering N* — neighbourhood depth on G in adjacent sampling; the running example uses N=1 (direct co-authors of the target paper's authors).
- *Prompt_Optimizer* — an LLM constructed by the authors that takes previous prompts, their five-dim scores, and structural constraints (XML format) and returns a refined prompt; the iterative-update step is analogised to policy optimisation (prompt = policy, modifications = actions, scores = rewards).

**7. Confusions / unresolved points (from `scientific-clarity-checker` audit).**
- *Self-preference in the LLM judge.* DeepSeek-V3 is both the GYWI backbone in the ablation and the LLM judge in Fig. 6. Strong evidence in the LLM-ideation literature that LLM judges prefer outputs from their own family. The paper does not control for this and does not run a leave-one-judge-out check or use a different judge family. Hedging on the Fig. 6 results should arguably be stronger than the paper allows.
- *Statistical-significance asymmetry.* The paired-t-test gate is applied carefully *inside* the prompt-update loop (α=0.05 over N_eval=50), but no statistical test is reported for the headline cross-LLM IMCQ comparison or for the ablation. The reader cannot assess whether 88.89 vs 89.90 (the +GraphRAG step) is meaningful.
- *Hop order N and sampling sizes.* The example uses N=1 and the formal `k_adj` / `k_rand` are described as adjustable hyperparameters, but the specific values used in the headline experiments are not disclosed in the main text. Reproducibility depends on numbers not stated.
- *Cross-LLM comparison vs ablation framing.* In Fig. 4 GYWI runs on DeepSeek-V3 and is compared to GPT-4o, Gemini 2.5, Qwen3-8B, DeepSeek-V3 as plain baselines — this conflates the GYWI scaffold with backbone strength. The authors' own interpretation of the ablation (Fig. 5) — that retrieval and graph contribute more than prompt optimisation — is plausible but is presented over a single backbone with no seed variance.
- *Definition of "non-collaborative pairs" in Table I.* Not stated; the 4.5x ratio rests on this definition.
- *Hedging calibration.* Claim language ("significantly outperforms mainstream LLMs in multiple metrics such as novelty, reliability, and relevance") is firmer than the underlying single-seed, single-domain, n=1,000 evaluation supports. The authors' own limitations section is appropriately hedged on cross-domain generalisation; the abstract and Fig. 6/7 discussions are less hedged.
- *"Eureka jump" claim for random sampling is qualitative.* No ablation on `k_rand=0` vs `k_adj=0` to show the two sampling modes are individually necessary; the design is asserted rather than empirically separated.
- *Scoring leakage risk.* The same five dimensions (novelty/feasibility/clarity/relevance/significance) are used both *inside* the prompt-optimisation loop's reward and *outside* by the LLM judge in Fig. 6. This raises a Goodhart concern: the prompt is RL-trained against exactly the metric on which it is later scored, by the same LLM family.
